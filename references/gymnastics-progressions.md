# Gymnastics Progressions

Teaching sequences, prerequisites, and regressions/progressions for the
gymnastics and bodyweight skills common in CrossFit. Used by
`crossfit-movement-progression-builder`, the fault analyzer, scaling, and
foundations skills. Progressions are guides, not guarantees — advancement is
earned by meeting criteria, not by calendar.

## General sequence for any skill

Prerequisite strength/position → static hold → controlled partial → full strict
range → kip/cycle efficiency → volume/under-fatigue → workout application.
Always confirm the **prerequisite** before chasing the kip; kipping a movement
the athlete cannot do strictly (or nearly) hides faults and adds risk.

## Pull-up family

- **Prerequisites:** active hang, scapular pull-ups (shrug/depress), hollow hold.
- **Regressions:** ring rows (feet position = difficulty), banded strict, eccentric
  (jump up / lower slow), partner-assisted.
- **Strict → kipping → butterfly:** earn 3–5 strict before kipping for volume;
  butterfly only once kipping is rhythmic and the shoulder tolerates volume.
- **Chest-to-bar / bar muscle-up:** require a strong, fast kipping pull and, for
  BMU, a confident transition (hip-to-bar contact, fast turnover) and dip
  strength. Ring muscle-up adds false-grip and ring stability demands.
- **Common faults:** open hollow (banana), early arm bend, no scap engagement,
  press-out/dip weakness on muscle-ups.

## Handstand & pressing

- **Prerequisites:** shoulder overhead mobility, wrist tolerance, hollow body,
  wall handstand hold.
- **Handstand push-up:** box/pike HSPU → kipping HSPU (with hip drive) → strict;
  control depth with ab-mat standards. Strict deficit and freestanding are
  advanced.
- **Handstand walk:** wall holds → shoulder taps → wall walks → short freestanding
  holds → travel. Limiter is usually overhead position + balance, not strength.
- **Common faults:** lumbar over-extension, elbows flaring, head leading,
  inconsistent hand placement.

## Core / midline

- **Toes-to-bar:** active hang + hollow → knee raises → toes-to-rings/feet to
  target → full T2B → cycling rhythm. Limiter is often lat/grip and hollow
  control, not abs alone.
- **GHD sit-up:** introduce **conservatively** with limited range and volume; it
  is a classic novel-stimulus rhabdo risk when over-dosed (see
  [safety-and-referral.md](safety-and-referral.md)).

## Other staples

- **Pistols:** ankle/hip mobility + single-leg strength; regress to box/assisted.
- **Rope climb:** grip + pulling + leg-lock technique; regress to floor
  lay-to-stand or strict pulls.
- **Double-unders:** single-under rhythm → "penguin" timing → DU; common fault is
  over-piking and arm-driven turns.

## Dosage & advancement

Program skills at a **frequency** that drives adaptation (often 2–4 short
exposures/week) with quality > quantity. Advance only when criteria are met
(e.g. "5 strict pull-ups, hollow held 30s" before kipping volume). Always pair a
new skill with a **retest** to confirm progress. See
[movement-pattern-taxonomy.md](movement-pattern-taxonomy.md).
