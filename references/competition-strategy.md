# Competition Strategy

Planning and in-event strategy for CrossFit-style competition. Used by
`crossfit-competition-preparation-planner` and
`crossfit-competition-workout-strategist`. **Standards and rules are
version-dependent** — never invent them; confirm the official source for the
specific event/season/division (see
[source-version-registry.json](source-version-registry.json) and the
`crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor` skill).

## Preparation (macro)

- **Identify the target:** event type (Open / online qualifier / local throwdown
  / sanctioned multi-event / team), date(s), division, and known format history.
- **Phase the prep:** base/accumulation → competition-specific → peak/taper.
  Bias the specific phase toward the event's likely demands (movements,
  standards, time domains, repeatability).
- **Standards exposure:** practice to the *actual* movement standards once
  confirmed (depth, lockout, no-rep criteria). Don't drill guessed standards.
- **Peak & taper:** reduce volume, maintain intensity/sharpness into the event;
  protect skills and positions.
- **Event recovery:** plan inter-event and inter-day recovery (multi-event days
  are a recovery-management problem).
- **Simulations:** rehearse competition-day logistics, warm-up windows, and
  back-to-back events. Manage **skill-risk** (don't gamble high-failure movements
  without a contingency).
- **Travel:** sleep, nutrition, equipment familiarity, schedule shifts.

## Workout strategy (micro — for a released workout)

Require the athlete to **supply or confirm the official workout description and
standards** before giving standard-dependent advice. Then:

1. **Interpret** the workout: format, flow, what it rewards.
2. **Confirm standards** and no-rep risks (the cheapest points are no-reps not
   given).
3. **Set a goal score / placement target** grounded in the athlete's capacities.
4. **Find bottlenecks** — the movement(s) that will gate the score.
5. **Rep-break strategy** — planned partitioning to avoid redline/failure
   (e.g. breaking gymnastics early to protect cycle rate).
6. **Pacing** — opening pace, where to push, where to hold.
7. **Transitions & setup** — bar/equipment layout, chalk, hydration, foot traffic.
8. **Failure points & contingencies** — what to do if a set fails or grip goes.
9. **Repeat decision** (for repeatable events) — whether/when to redo and what to
   change.

## Honesty rules

Never fabricate standards, scoring tables, or penalties. If the official
description is unavailable, provide a **clearly labeled conditional** strategy
("assuming standard chest-to-bar and a 12-min cap…") and tell the athlete which
facts to confirm with event staff. See
[coaching-principles.md](coaching-principles.md) §10.
