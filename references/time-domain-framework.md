# Time-Domain Framework

Time domains organize the **energy-system stimulus** of conditioning. Choosing the
domain is how you control intensity intent. Used by the designer, cycle builder,
auditor, and scaling skills.

## Domains and their dominant stimulus

| Domain | Duration | Dominant energy system | Intended experience | Typical formats |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sprint / power | <2 min | Phosphagen / alactic | Near-max output, full or long recovery | heavy singles, short sprints, max-effort intervals |
| Short metcon | 2–7 min | Glycolytic ("the pain") | High, sustainable-just-barely, redline risk | "Fran"-style couplets/triplets |
| Medium metcon | 7–15 min | Mixed glycolytic/aerobic | Hard but pace-managed; can't sprint, can't coast | classic triplets, moderate chippers |
| Long metcon | 15–30 min | Aerobic dominant | Sustained "conversational-hard" grind, steady pace | longer chippers, aerobic intervals |
| Aerobic / endurance | 30+ min | Aerobic / oxidative | Steady-state, repeatable, low redline | long monostructural, partner long pieces |
| Intervals | varies | Targeted (work:rest ratio sets the system) | Repeatable quality at a set effort | EMOM, on/off pieces, capacity intervals |

## Using work:rest to target a system

- **Phosphagen/power:** short work, long rest (e.g. :10–:30 work, 1:5+ rest) to
  keep each rep near-max.
- **Glycolytic:** moderate work, incomplete rest (1:1 to 1:3) to accumulate
  lactate tolerance — sparingly; high cost.
- **Aerobic:** longer work, short rest (2:1+ to continuous) at a repeatable pace.

## Programming rules

- A complete cycle touches **all** domains over time; a week that is all 8–12 min
  triplets is not "varied," it is monotone glycolytic exposure. The auditor flags
  missing domains.
- **Match movements and loads to the domain.** Heavy barbell cycling does not
  belong in a true long aerobic piece if the goal is steady pacing; it converts
  the stimulus to interval/grind.
- **Pacing guidance is part of the prescription.** Tell the athlete the intended
  pace ("should be able to hold unbroken until round 4", "sprint, redline okay")
  so the domain's stimulus is actually realized.
- **Recovery and impact scale with intensity.** Back-to-back redline glycolytic
  days are a recovery problem, not extra fitness. See
  [coaching-principles.md](coaching-principles.md) §4.

## Selecting a domain from a goal

- Build the engine / GPP base → bias medium-long aerobic with interval support.
- Improve "the sprint"/threshold → targeted short metcon + glycolytic intervals,
  rationed.
- Raise power/strength expression → phosphagen work + strength block (see
  [weightlifting-progressions.md](weightlifting-progressions.md)).
