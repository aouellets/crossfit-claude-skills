# Weightlifting & Strength Progressions

Teaching sequences and programming guidance for the Olympic lifts and primary
barbell strength in a mixed-modal context. Used by the progression builder,
specialty-cycle builder, cycle builder, and fault analyzer. Olympic lifting and
absolute strength respond to **structured progression**, not random daily
variation (see [coaching-principles.md](coaching-principles.md) §5).

## Olympic lifts — teaching sequence

Teach positions top-down or via the burgener-style sequence; confirm overhead and
front-rack mobility first.

- **Snatch:** overhead position/stability → overhead squat → snatch balance →
  hang (high → low) → from floor → full. Sub-skills: snatch grip deadlift,
  pulls, drops.
- **Clean & jerk:** front rack/squat → power clean from hang → full clean →
  push press → push jerk → split jerk. Sub-skills: clean pulls, front squats.
- **Common faults:** early arm pull, bar swinging out, slow turnover, soft
  receiving position, jumping forward, pressing out the jerk.

## Strength progression models (pick by training age)

- **Novice:** linear progression — add small load each session while form holds;
  retest by simply continuing to add. Fastest returns; abandon when stalls
  repeat.
- **Intermediate:** weekly undulation or wave loading (e.g. 5/3/1-style, or
  heavy/medium/light across the week); progress week-to-week, deload ~every
  4–6 weeks.
- **Advanced:** block/conjugate approaches; longer accumulation→intensification→
  peak structures with planned tapers.

Express loads as **%1RM or RPE/RIR** tied to the block's intent, not arbitrary
numbers. Avoid automatic maximal testing; test when the plan calls for a retest.

## Integrating strength with conditioning (interference)

- Sequence to protect the priority: heavy strength **before** fatiguing
  conditioning when strength is the goal; separate sessions when possible.
- Manage shared-pattern fatigue (heavy squats + high-volume wall balls/box jumps
  compound knee/quad load).
- Use a **deload** every few weeks; track bar speed/RPE to catch overreaching.

## Accessory & positional work

Program accessories to fix the **limiter** (e.g. front-rack mobility, midline,
posterior chain, overhead stability), not generic bodybuilding filler. Tie each
accessory to the fault it addresses (see the fault analyzer) and retest the
position.

## Retesting

Every strength/Oly block ends with a planned retest (a top single/triple at RPE,
or a tested max only when justified). Compare to baseline; let the result set the
next block. See [gymnastics-progressions.md](gymnastics-progressions.md) for the
bodyweight-skill analog.
