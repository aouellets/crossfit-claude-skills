## Target & pattern
Bar muscle-up — **vertical pull → press transition** (gymnastics; see
gymnastics-progressions). Combines a fast kipping pull, hip-to-bar contact, fast
turnover, and a dip press-out.

## Prerequisites
Fast, strong kipping pull (ideally **chest-to-bar** reps), confident hollow/arch
kip, and **dip strength** (can press out of the bottom of a dip). Without C2B and
a dip, the muscle-up will not turn over.

## Assessment tests
- Max chest-to-bar pull-ups (need height: hips/chest to bar).
- Strict dip / banded dip for reps (press-out strength).
- Kip swing control: tight hollow→arch without piking.

## Limiting factors
Usually **turnover speed + dip press-out**, sometimes timing/fear at the bar.

## Teaching sequence
Kipping pull → high chest-to-bar (get hips/chest to the bar) → banded/jumping bar
muscle-up (groove turnover) → low-volume bar muscle-up → for reps.

## Drills, regressions & progressions
- **High C2B for height:** pull to sternum, not chin (fault: pulling late/low).
- **Banded bar muscle-up:** band assists turnover; drill the elbow-fast catch.
- **Box/jumping MU:** removes pull demand, isolates the transition + dip.
- **Negatives:** slow lower from support through the transition.

## Accessory work
Dips (banded → strict) for press-out; straight-arm/scap pulls and hollow work for
a tight kip. Targeted to the turnover/dip limiter.

## Weekly dosage
2–3 short, fresh exposures/week; transition and dip drills are quality-limited —
stop when turnover slows.

## Advancement criteria
- **To attempt the MU:** consistent **high chest-to-bar** (sternum to bar) + a
  **strict or lightly banded dip**.
- **To do reps:** 3 singles with clean turnover and no press-out grind.

## Common faults
Pulling too low, slow turnover, soft/incomplete dip lockout, leaning back instead
of fast hip-to-bar contact.

## Retest
Retest C2B height and dip strength in 4–6 weeks; once both gates are met, retest a
single bar muscle-up attempt. Result sets the next block.
