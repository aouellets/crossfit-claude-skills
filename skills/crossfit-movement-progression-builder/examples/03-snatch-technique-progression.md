# Example 3 — Snatch technique progression

**Prompt:** "Help me get my snatch up — technique is holding me back, not strength."

**Context:** Intermediate, snatch 1RM 60 kg, back squat 130 kg, overhead position
"feels okay", has a full barbell and rack.

---

## Target & pattern
Snatch — **Olympic / triple-extension power** (see weightlifting-progressions).
A snatch far below squat strength (60 vs 130) confirms it's **technical/positional**,
not leg strength.

## Prerequisites
Overhead position + stability (overhead squat at a meaningful load), confident
receiving position, and front-rack/shoulder mobility. Verify overhead squat first.

## Assessment tests
- Overhead squat for a heavy triple — does the bar drift or the chest fold?
- Snatch-balance speed (how fast can you drop under?).
- Hang-snatch vs full-snatch gap (positional vs first-pull issue).

## Limiting factors
Likely **turnover speed + receiving position + bar path**; overhead stability is
the gate, given strong legs.

## Teaching sequence
Overhead position → overhead squat → snatch balance → hang (high → low) → from
floor → full. Sub-skills: snatch-grip deadlift, snatch pulls, drops.

## Drills, regressions & progressions
- **Snatch balance 3×3:** build speed under and a confident catch (fault: pressing out).
- **Hang power → hang squat snatch:** groove turnover without the floor's variables.
- **Tall/muscle snatch:** drill fast elbows and bar-close path (fault: bar swinging out front).
- **Snatch pulls @ 90–100%:** reinforce extension and bar path.

## Accessory work
Overhead-squat strength and shoulder stability (the overhead limiter); midline for
position. Tied to position, not generic volume.

## Weekly dosage
2–3 snatch sessions/week; express loads as **%1RM / RPE** tied to intent
(technique work ~70–80%, RPE 6–7), heavier pulls separately. Deload ~every 4–6
weeks. **No automatic max testing** — test only at the planned retest.

## Advancement criteria
- **Hang → floor:** consistent catch at ~80% from the hang with no press-out.
- **Technique → load:** clean bar path and fast turnover at RPE 7 before chasing PRs.

## Common faults
Early arm pull, bar swinging out, slow turnover, soft/forward receiving position,
pressing out the catch.

## Retest
Retest a snatch single at RPE 8–9 after ~6 weeks (a tested max only if justified);
compare to 60 kg and let the result set the next block.
