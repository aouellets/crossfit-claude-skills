# Example 1 — Strict → kipping → butterfly pull-up plan (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Help me build my pull-ups. I want kipping and eventually butterfly."

**Context:** Can do 2 strict pull-ups, no shoulder pain, has a rig and bands.

---

## Target & pattern
Pull-up family — **vertical pull** (see movement-pattern-taxonomy). Goal:
unbroken kipping pull-ups, then butterfly.

## Prerequisites
Active hang, scapular pull-ups (shrug/depress), a 30 s hollow hold, and
**3–5 strict pull-ups** before adding kipping volume. The athlete currently owns
the hang and ~2 strict — so the first block earns strict strength + hollow.

## Assessment tests
- Max strict pull-ups (today: 2).
- Hollow hold for time (test now).
- Scap pull-up control: 5 clean reps?
- Active-hang shoulder tolerance: 30 s pain-free?

## Limiting factors
Most likely **pulling strength + midline (hollow) control**, not technique.
Butterfly is gated behind a rhythmic kip, which is gated behind strict strength.

## Teaching sequence
Active hang → scap pull-ups → strict (assisted → full) → hollow/arch (kip swing)
→ kip + 1 pull-up → kipping for reps → butterfly rhythm → butterfly volume.

## Drills, regressions & progressions
- **Eccentrics:** jump to top, 3–5 s lower × 5 (builds strict — fault: early arm bend).
- **Banded strict:** 4×5 with the lightest band that lets you finish clean.
- **Ring rows:** feet-forward to scale difficulty; 4×8 for horizontal pull volume.
- **Hollow + arch holds → kip swing:** groove the swing only once strict is near 5.

## Accessory work
Hollow holds and tempo ring rows (midline + scap control), banded face-pulls for
shoulder health under coming volume. All tied to the strict/midline limiter.

## Weekly dosage
3 short skill exposures/week, fresh (not after a hard metcon). ~10–15 quality
reps of the priority drill per session; stop sets before form degrades.

## Advancement criteria
- **Strict → kip:** **5 strict pull-ups + 30 s hollow** before kipping for volume.
- **Kip → butterfly:** rhythmic, unbroken kipping of 8–10 with no shoulder
  irritation before introducing butterfly.

## Common faults
Open hollow (banana), early arm bend, no scap engagement, rushing to kip before
strict strength exists (hides faults, raises shoulder risk).

## Retest
Re-test max strict and hollow in 4 weeks; expect strict to roughly double from 2
toward 4–5. When the strict gate is met, retest kip rhythm and start the butterfly
stage. The retest sets the next block.
