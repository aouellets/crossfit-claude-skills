# Example 3 — Provisional plan when key inputs are missing

**Prompt:** "Can you write me a programming cycle?"

**Context:** No goal, no schedule, no level, no equipment, no maxes given.

---

> **PROVISIONAL PLAN — built on stated assumptions.** Confirm the two questions at
> the end and I will tighten the architecture and loads.

## Athlete & goal
Assumed: **broad GPP**, no single dominant weakness, recreational-to-intermediate.

## Assumptions (each labeled — none fabricated as fact)
- **Schedule:** assumed **4 sessions/week for 4 weeks** (a safe default block).
- **Level:** assumed intermediate; loads given as **RPE**, not %1RM, because no
  maxes were provided — I will not invent a back-squat number.
- **Equipment:** assumed a standard box (barbell, rack, rower or bike, pull-up rig,
  dumbbells/kettlebells). Bodyweight fallbacks noted where it matters.

## Architecture
- **Model:** RPE-anchored, lightly undulating; one deload-lite in wk4.
- **Distribution:** 1 strength-bias / 1 skill + short metcon / 1 longer aerobic /
  1 mixed-modal.

## Weekly structure (representative)

| Day | Focus | Key prescription (RPE-based until maxes confirmed) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Day 1 | Strength + short metcon | Back squat 5×5 @ RPE 7 (leave 3 reps in tank); 5–7 min couplet |
| Day 2 | Skill + medium metcon | Pull-up/T2B skill EMOM (quality); 10–12 min triplet, pace-managed |
| Day 3 | Long aerobic | 25–30 min Z2 row/bike, conversational-hard |
| Day 4 | Mixed-modal | 12–15 min chipper, balanced patterns, RPE 7–8 |

## Progression criteria
Add small load while RPE holds (e.g. squat: same RPE, slightly more load each week).
Convert RPE targets to %1RM once maxes are provided.

## Testing & retesting
Pick one strength marker (squat top set at RPE) and one conditioning benchmark in
wk1; retest both in wk4. No automatic 1RM testing.

## Adjustment rules
If RPE 7 targets feel like RPE 9, the assumed level is too high — drop volume one
notch and tell me.

## Recovery
Wk4 lighter. Sleep/protein assumed adequate.

## Risks & referral
Proportionate stop conditions. If any pain limits movement, this is not the right
skill — refer and route to `crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`.

## Two questions that change everything
1. **How many days/week and for how many weeks** can you train?
2. **What is the goal** — broad fitness, a benchmark, a lift, a skill, or an event?
   (And your current key maxes, if you have them, so I can switch RPE to %1RM.)
