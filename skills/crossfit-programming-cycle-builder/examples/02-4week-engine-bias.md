# Example 2 — 4-week engine-bias block (goal-biased cycle)

**Prompt:** "I gas out in longer workouts. Give me a 4-week block to fix my engine.
4 days a week."

**Context:** Intermediate, full gym including rower + bike. Strength is fine; aerobic
capacity is the limiter. 5 km row ~22:30 (slow for level). No injuries.

---

## Athlete & goal
Intermediate; primary adaptation = **aerobic capacity / threshold** (engine bias),
controlling interference with existing strength. 4 weeks, 4 sessions/week.

## Assumptions
- Strength maintained, not progressed, this block (one heavy day to retain).
- ~60 min sessions; rower + bike available.
- "Gas out" = aerobic + threshold limiter, not pacing skill alone. If it is pacing,
  the engine work doubles as pacing practice.

## Architecture
- **Model:** aerobic base build with rationed threshold intervals; one maintenance
  strength day so squat/press don't detrain.
- **Phases:** wk1–3 progressive aerobic build → wk4 partial deload + retest.
- **Distribution:** 1 long aerobic / 1 threshold-interval / 1 mixed-modal / 1
  strength-maintenance.

## Weekly structure (representative)

| Day | Focus | Key prescription |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mon | Long aerobic (Z2) | Row/bike 35→45 min across weeks @ conversational-hard pace; nasal-breathing check |
| Wed | Threshold intervals | 5×4 min @ ~85% effort / 2 min easy (wk1) → 5×5 min / 2 min (wk3); rationed glycolytic exposure |
| Fri | Mixed-modal aerobic | 20–25 min long metcon, low-skill movements so the engine is the limiter, not technique |
| Sat | Strength maintenance | Back squat 3×3 @ 80% (maintain), short accessory; no fatiguing metcon after |

## Progression criteria
- Long day: add ~5 min/week at the same pace, or hold time and raise pace target.
- Intervals: extend work interval (4→5 min) before adding intensity; keep the
  work:rest near 2:1 to stay aerobic-threshold, not all-out.
- Advance only if the prior week's pace was held to the last interval.

## Testing & retesting
- **Baseline (wk1):** 5 km row time trial (22:30), and a 10-min max-distance bike.
- **Retest (wk4):** repeat both. Target a measurable 5 km improvement (~30–60 s is
  realistic in 4 weeks for an intermediate). No max strength test this block.

## Adjustment rules
- If pace collapses on the last interval two weeks running → reduce interval count,
  hold pace; the engine is under-recovered.
- If strength markers drop on the maintenance day → that day is too fatigued; move
  it away from the threshold day.

## Recovery
Threshold intervals are costly — only one such day/week. Wk4 cuts interval volume
~40% before the retest. Sleep prioritized; the long day is meant to be repeatable,
not redline.

## Risks & referral
Proportionate: stop for chest pain, dizziness, or unusual shortness of breath
(refer, don't push). Aerobic bias is low-impact by design. No outcome guarantees.

## Rationale
Aerobic capacity responds to volume at a repeatable pace plus rationed threshold
work; the single strength day prevents detraining without competing for recovery
with the engine work (interference management per coaching-principles).
