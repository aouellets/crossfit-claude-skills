# Example 2 — Squat depth: ankle restriction vs. motor control

**Prompt:** "I can't hit depth in the squat — my heels come up and I tip forward.
No pain. Is it my ankles or am I doing it wrong?"

**Context:** Intermediate, no pain, has a wall, a plate, and a barbell.

---

## Limitation
Cannot reach a below-parallel squat with heels down and an upright-enough torso;
heels lift and the torso tips forward. **No pain reported.**

## Likely cause
The two common drivers here are **passive ankle dorsiflexion restriction** and
**motor control** (sequencing/bracing/foot pressure). The test below separates them.

## Discriminating test
**Knee-to-wall ankle test vs. heel-elevated squat test:**
- *Knee-to-wall:* foot facing a wall, drive the knee forward over the toes keeping
  the heel down; measure the toe-to-wall distance at which the knee still touches.
  - < ~10 cm (4 in) on one or both sides → **passive ankle restriction**.
  - ≥ ~10 cm and symmetrical → ankle range is likely sufficient → look at motor
    control.
- *Heel-elevated squat:* squat with heels on a small plate.
  - Depth and torso instantly improve → confirms **ankle**-driven limit.
  - Still tips forward / heels-up feeling even elevated → **motor control**
    (bracing, foot tripod, hip sequencing).

## Targeted plan
- **If ankle:** banded ankle dorsiflexion mobilization 2 × 30 s/side; calf/soleus
  stretch 2 × 45 s; weighted knee-to-wall reps 2 × 10/side. 5–6 days/week, pre-squat.
  Interim: squat with a small heel lift while range improves.
- **If motor control:** goblet-squat holds (active depth, knees out, big brace)
  3 × 20 s; tempo box squats to a depth target 4 × 5 (3-s descent); foot-tripod /
  brace cueing every working set. 3–4 days/week.
- Pain-free range only.

## Objective retest
- Knee-to-wall: reach ≥ 10 cm, symmetrical — **re-screen in 3 weeks**.
- Air squat filmed side-on: hip crease clearly below the knee with heels down — same
  camera position at 3 weeks.

## Safety
No pain reported → mobility screen. **Stop and re-route** if a drill provokes sharp
ankle, knee, or hip pain, pinching, or instability (refer +
`crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`,
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)).
This screen classifies a *position* limit; it does not diagnose any joint tissue.
