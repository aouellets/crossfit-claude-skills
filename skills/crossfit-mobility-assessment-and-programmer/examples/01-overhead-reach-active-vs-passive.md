# Example 1 — Overhead reach limited: active vs. passive (happy path)

**Prompt:** "I can't get my arms fully overhead — my overhead squat and snatch
catch feel jammed and I lean forward. No pain, just stiff. How do I fix it?"

**Context:** Intermediate, no pain, has a barbell, dowel, and a wall.

---

## Limitation
Cannot reach a fully vertical overhead position; shows up as a forward lean and a
"jammed" feeling in the overhead squat and snatch catch. **No pain reported.**

## Likely cause
Most likely a mix of **passive lat / thoracic-spine restriction** and **active
mobility** (can't own the top range under control). The discriminating test below
separates them; on the evidence so far I will not assert a single cause.

## Discriminating test
**Supine overhead reach (passive) vs. standing wall slide (active):**
- *Supine, knees bent, low back flat:* raise straight arms overhead and try to touch
  the floor without the ribs flaring or back arching.
  - Arms reach the floor easily → passive range is fine → the limit is **active /
    motor control**.
  - Arms stop short even relaxed → **passive restriction** (lat / t-spine).
- *Standing wall slide:* back/head/arms against the wall, slide arms up keeping
  contact.
  - Loses wall contact early but the supine test was clean → confirms **active**.

## Targeted plan
Matched to the test result:
- **If passive:** lat stretch on a rig 2 × 45 s/side; thoracic extension over a
  foam roller 2 × 8 slow reps; banded overhead distraction 2 × 30 s/side. 5–6
  days/week, in warm-up.
- **If active/motor-control:** wall slides 3 × 8; prone/standing "Y" raises 3 × 10;
  light dowel overhead holds (active reach, ribs down) 3 × 20 s. 4–5 days/week.
- Either way, finish with **loaded carryover**: 3 × 5 tempo overhead-position holds
  with an empty/light barbell, ribs down — practicing the position you want.
Work strictly in a **pain-free range**.

## Objective retest
- Supine overhead reach: hands touch the floor, ribs down — **re-screen in 3 weeks**.
- Overhead squat with a dowel: torso noticeably more vertical (film side-on,
  same camera spot) at the 3-week mark.

## Safety
No pain reported, so this is a mobility screen. **Stop and re-route** if any drill
provokes sharp shoulder pain, pinching, numbness, or tingling — that would make this
a pain-limited case (refer + `crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`,
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)).
This screen names a *position* limit; it does not diagnose any shoulder tissue.
