# Example 1 — 6-session small-group on-ramp (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Design a 6-session beginner on-ramp for small groups at our box."

**Context:** New athletes, no notable injuries, full equipment, ~4–6 per group.

---

## Course architecture (6 sessions × topics)
| Session | Primary patterns / lifts | Gymnastics | Mono | Culture |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Air squat, hinge/deadlift setup | hollow/active hang | row/bike intro | terminology, whiteboard |
| 2 | Front squat, press | ring rows, scaled push-up | run/row pacing | scaling intro |
| 3 | Deadlift load, sumo deadlift high pull | hanging knee raise | EMOM format | etiquette, equipment care |
| 4 | Overhead press → push press | scaled pull-up paths | AMRAP format | scoring / RX vs scaled |
| 5 | Clean (from hang), thruster | toes-to-bar scale | mixed couplet | intensity, stop conditions |
| 6 | Integrate + first scaled WOD | review | review | recovery, next steps |

Format: small-group. Total budget: 6 sessions. **Progression is gated by the
checkpoints below, not by having attended 6 times.**

## Per-session plan (objective · key movements · competence checkpoint)
1. **Squat & hinge mechanics.** Air squat, deadlift setup. *Checkpoint:* air squat
   to depth with neutral spine; hinge without rounding under an empty bar.
2. **Front rack & press.** Front squat, strict press. *Checkpoint:* stable front
   rack; press without rib flare.
3. **Loaded hinge & pulling.** Deadlift, SDHP, ring rows. *Checkpoint:* deadlift
   moderate load with neutral spine; complete an EMOM at a controlled pace.
4. **Overhead & pull-up paths.** Push press, scaled pull-up. *Checkpoint:* push
   press with stable midline; chooses an appropriate pull-up scale.
5. **Clean & intensity.** Hang clean, thruster. *Checkpoint:* hang clean mechanics
   under light load; self-scales a couplet and states two stop conditions.
6. **Integration.** First scaled WOD. *Checkpoint:* completes a scaled WOD safely,
   self-scaling, and reads the whiteboard/score.

## Scaling & safety intro
Athletes are taught to self-scale (load, reps, range, movement substitution) to
hold the workout's intent, and to **stop for sharp joint pain, dizziness, or form
breakdown**. Rhabdomyolysis caution is named in session 5: new athletes must not
chase volume; dark urine or severe disproportionate soreness warrants medical
evaluation. See
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).

## Culture coverage
Terminology (AMRAP, EMOM, RX, rounds/reps), whiteboard and scoring, etiquette
(share platforms, wipe equipment, re-rack), and basic recovery (sleep, soreness vs.
pain).

## Graduation criteria (competence-based)
Ready for regular class when the athlete meets **every** checkpoint above and can
self-scale a mixed WOD. **If not yet met:** repeat the relevant session(s) or add a
1:1 — do not graduate on attendance alone.

## Format variants
- **1:1:** same six checkpoints, often in 3–4 longer sessions.
- **Accelerated:** only if competence is demonstrated faster — never by skipping the
  mechanics phase.
- **Multiweek group:** spread the six over 2–3 weeks with extra practice volume.
