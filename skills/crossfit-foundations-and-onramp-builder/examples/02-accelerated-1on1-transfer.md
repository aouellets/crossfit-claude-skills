# Example 2 — Accelerated 2-session 1:1 for an experienced transfer

**Prompt:** "An experienced lifter is transferring from another gym. Can we do a
fast 2-session 1:1 on-ramp instead of the full course?"

**Context:** Strong barbell background, says they 'know the lifts'; new to
mixed-modal formats. Full equipment.

---

## Course architecture (2 sessions × topics, competence-gated)
| Session | Verify / teach | Checkpoint |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Verify squat/press/deadlift/clean mechanics; teach our standards | demonstrate each lift to our ROM/standards |
| 2 | Teach formats, scaling, scoring, etiquette; first scaled WOD | self-scale a couplet; read the whiteboard |

Format: accelerated 1:1. **Acceleration is conditional on demonstrated
competence** — if the verification in session 1 reveals gaps, we add sessions
rather than wave them through. The mechanics phase is verified, not skipped.

## Per-session plan
1. **Verification + standards.** Don't assume the lifts transfer cleanly: watch
   each lift, correct to our movement standards, and confirm overhead positions
   are sound before any intensity. *Checkpoint:* every key lift meets our ROM/
   standard at a working load.
2. **Formats & integration.** Teach AMRAP/EMOM/RX-vs-scaled, scoring, etiquette,
   and self-scaling; run one scaled WOD. *Checkpoint:* self-scales appropriately,
   states stop conditions, completes the WOD safely.

## Scaling & safety intro
Even an experienced athlete learns **our** scaling language and stop conditions.
Emphasize that new formats can produce an unfamiliar high-volume stimulus — pace
the first WODs and watch for rhabdo warning signs despite a strong background. See
[../../../references/coaching-principles.md](../../../references/coaching-principles.md)
§2 (mechanics → consistency → intensity).

## Culture coverage
Our terminology, whiteboard/scoring conventions, and etiquette specific to the box.

## Graduation criteria (competence-based)
Graduates after session 2 **only if** both checkpoints are met. A gap in lift
mechanics or format understanding adds a session — experience is not a substitute
for demonstrated competence to our standards.

## Format variants
- **If gaps appear:** expand to a 4-session path covering the missed competencies.
- **Group accelerated:** same two-checkpoint structure for a small group of
  transfers, verified individually.
