**Prompt:** "Give me a competence checklist I can use to decide when a new athlete

**Context:** Format, session count, and equipment not specified.

## Note on missing inputs (provisional)
**Assumption:** a standard small-group on-ramp at a fully equipped box. This
checklist is **provisional** and format-agnostic — it travels across 1:1,
accelerated, and multiweek paths because it gates on competence, not sessions. The
one field that would refine it: any **population/medical considerations** (which
would route to `crossfit-population-adaptation-coach`).

## Graduation competence checklist (competence-based)
An athlete is ready for regular class when they can demonstrate **all** of:

**Movement competence**
- Air squat and front/overhead positions to ROM with neutral spine.
- Deadlift / hinge a moderate load with a maintained midline.
- Press / push press overhead with a stable midline.
- Hang clean mechanics at a light-to-moderate load.
- A chosen, appropriate scale for pull-ups and toes-to-bar.

**Self-management competence**
- Self-scales load, reps, range, or movement to hold a workout's intent.
- States real stop conditions (sharp joint pain, dizziness, form breakdown) and
  recognizes rhabdomyolysis warning signs. See
  [../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).

**Culture competence**
- Uses core terminology (AMRAP, EMOM, RX/scaled), reads the whiteboard, logs a
  score, and follows etiquette (sharing, cleaning, re-racking).

## If a box is not yet met
Do **not** graduate on attendance. Repeat the relevant session, add a 1:1 for the
specific gap, or extend the timeline. Competence is the gate — see
[../../../references/coaching-principles.md](../../../references/coaching-principles.md)
§2 and §5.

## Using this across formats
Drop the same checklist into a 1:1, an accelerated transfer path, or a multiweek
group on-ramp — only the number of sessions to reach it changes, never the bar.
