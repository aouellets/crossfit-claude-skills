# Expected behaviors — CrossFit Foundations and Onramp Builder

## Always
- Produce a course architecture (sessions × topics) that covers patterns, key
  lifts, gymnastics, monostructural, formats, intensity, scaling, safety,
  etiquette, terminology, scoring, and recovery before graduation.
- Give per-session objectives, key movements, and a concrete competence checkpoint.
- Sequence movement teaching mechanics-first, respecting prerequisites.
- Gate progression and graduation on demonstrated competence, not attendance.
- Teach scaling, stop conditions, and rhabdomyolysis caution as explicit content.
- Offer 1:1 / accelerated / multiweek variants of the same competencies.

## Never
- Graduate an athlete on attendance count alone.
- Skip the mechanics phase, even on an accelerated path or for an experienced
  transfer — verify competence instead of assuming it.
- Introduce a movement before its prerequisite (e.g. clean before deadlift).
- Promise that an on-ramp guarantees competitive results or any outcome.
- Build ongoing post-graduation programming, special-population adaptation, or a
  single-session minute-by-minute plan — route those out.
- Fabricate athlete history or competence.

## Routing
- Ongoing programming after graduation → `crossfit-programming-cycle-builder`.
- Special populations / pregnancy / postpartum / masters / deconditioned →
  `crossfit-population-adaptation-coach`.
- Minute-by-minute single-session timing → `crossfit-class-plan-builder`.
- Extended single-skill build → `crossfit-movement-progression-builder`.
- Pain / injury / red flags → safety-and-referral first, then
  `crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`.

## Safety
- New athletes are high-risk for too-much-too-soon: enforce mechanics →
  consistency → intensity and conservative early volume.
- Refuse requests to bypass foundations or to put a brand-new athlete straight into
  the hardest class; explain the progression and the risk.
- Escalate red-flag symptoms per the safety framework.
