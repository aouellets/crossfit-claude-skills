# Rubric — CrossFit Foundations and Onramp Builder

Score each response 0 (absent) / 1 (partial) / 2 (solid). A passing response
scores ≥ 1 on every dimension and 2 on Competence-gating and Safety sequencing.

## 1. Course architecture
- Maps sessions × topics; states format and total session/time budget.
- Covers every required area before graduation: patterns, key lifts, gymnastics,
  monostructural, formats, intensity, scaling, safety, etiquette, terminology,
  scoring, recovery.

## 2. Per-session design
- Each session has a clear objective, the key movements introduced, and a concrete
  competence checkpoint.
- Movement teaching is sequenced mechanics-first with prerequisites respected
  (e.g. deadlift before clean, air squat before overhead).

## 3. Competence-gating (critical)
- Progression and graduation are gated on **demonstrated competence, not
  attendance count**.
- If a checkpoint is not met, the response repeats/extends rather than graduating.

## 4. Safety sequencing (critical)
- Applies mechanics → consistency → intensity; intensity is introduced last.
- Teaches stop conditions and rhabdomyolysis caution for new athletes.
- Never skips the mechanics phase, even on an accelerated path; verifies rather
  than assumes competence for transfers.

## 5. Culture & self-management coverage
- Teaches etiquette, terminology, scoring/whiteboard, and how athletes self-scale.

## 6. Incomplete-information handling
- When format/sessions/equipment are missing, returns a clearly labeled
  provisional course with a stated assumption, keeping checkpoints as the real
  gate; never fabricates athlete history or competence.

## 7. Routing discipline
- Routes ongoing programming to `crossfit-programming-cycle-builder`, special
  populations to `crossfit-population-adaptation-coach`, single-session timing to
  `crossfit-class-plan-builder`, and extended single-skill builds to
  `crossfit-movement-progression-builder`. Refuses outcome guarantees.
