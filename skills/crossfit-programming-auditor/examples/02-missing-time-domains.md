# Example 2 — "All my metcons are 8–12 min" missing-domains audit

**Prompt:** "Critique my last cycle — I feel like I've plateaued on my engine."

**Context (supplied program):** A 4-week block. Every conditioning piece is an
8–12 minute triplet (AMRAP or for-time). No sessions under 7 min, none over 15
min, no dedicated strength progression, no intervals. Movements rotate but the
duration never does.

---

## Program snapshot
| Week | Metcon durations | Domains present | Strength block |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1–4 | 8, 10, 12, 9, 11, 8 … (all 8–12 min) | Medium metcon only | None structured |

Time-domain coverage: **medium metcon only.** Sprint/power, short glycolytic,
long aerobic, and pure aerobic/endurance are all **absent**.

## Findings (ranked by severity)
| Severity | Finding | Evidence (your program) | Consequence | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| High | Single time domain trained for a full block | Every conditioning piece is 8–12 min medium-domain | Monotone glycolytic-aerobic exposure; the energy systems you don't train (alactic power, threshold, long aerobic base) plateau — matches your reported engine stall | Spread across domains: see fix in revised week |
| High | No long aerobic / base work | Nothing over 15 min | Aerobic base — the foundation that raises recovery between intervals — is never built | Add a 25–35 min steady aerobic piece weekly (see the time-domain-framework reference) |
| Medium | No interval (work:rest) work | No EMOM/on-off pieces | Can't develop repeatable threshold quality; everything is "redline once" | Add a weekly interval session with defined work:rest |
| Medium | No structured strength progression | Strength is incidental to metcons | Power/strength expression stalls; metcon loads can't rise | Add one progressing lift with retests (see the weightlifting-progressions reference) |
| Low | Movement variety masking stimulus sameness | Movements change every day but duration doesn't | "Constantly varied" is being applied to movements, not to the conditioning stimulus — see coaching-principles §1 | Vary the domain, not just the exercises |

## Top corrections
1. **Vary the time domain, not just the movements.** A varied-looking block can still be a monotone energy-system block.
2. **Build an aerobic base** with one long steady piece per week.
3. **Add a strength progression** so loads — and therefore metcon intensity — can climb.

## Revised example week
- Mon: **Sprint/power** — 6 × 200 m run, full recovery (alactic) + strength: back squat 5×3 progressing
- Tue: **Short glycolytic** — "Fran"-style couplet, 2–7 min (sparingly)
- Wed: **Long aerobic** — 30 min steady row/bike/run at conversational-hard pace
- Thu: Rest / mobility
- Fri: **Interval** — EMOM 20 alternating engine + skill (work:rest ~1:1)
- Sat: **Medium metcon** — one of your 10–12 min triplets (keep what you do well)

This single week now spans sprint, short, medium, long, and interval domains — the spread your block was missing.

## Summary
You varied movements but never varied duration, so you trained one energy system for a month — which is exactly why the engine plateaued. Distribute time domains across the week and add an aerobic base plus a strength progression. Retest a benchmark engine piece (e.g. a 2 km row) at the end of the next block.
