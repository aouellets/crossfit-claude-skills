# Example 1 — Squat-heavy / no-pull imbalance audit (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Audit my last training week, here it is."

**Context (supplied program):**
- Mon: Back squat 5×5 @ 80% (1RM 140 kg); metcon — 21-15-9 thrusters (43 kg) + box jumps
- Tue: Front squat 4×4 @ 75%; metcon — 5 RFT 10 wall balls, 15 air squats, 200 m run
- Wed: Rest
- Thu: Overhead squat 4×3; metcon — AMRAP 12 of pistols + double-unders
- Fri: Back squat 3×3 @ 85%; metcon — "Karen" (150 wall balls)
- Sat: Long run 5 km

---

## Program snapshot
| Day | Strength pattern | Metcon patterns | Domain |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mon | Squat | Squat (thruster), jump | Short metcon (~5 min) |
| Tue | Squat | Squat, monostructural | Short metcon (~6 min) |
| Thu | Squat | Squat (pistol), jump | Medium (12 min) |
| Fri | Squat | Squat (wall ball) | Short–medium |
| Sat | — | Monostructural run | Long |

Total identifiable **pulling** volume (vertical or horizontal pull, hinge): **zero**.

## Findings (ranked by severity)
| Severity | Finding | Evidence (your program) | Consequence | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Critical | No hip-hinge or pulling pattern all week | Every strength slot is a squat variant; no deadlift, clean, swing, pull-up, or row appears | Posterior-chain and grip/pull capacity detrain; squat/hinge and push/pull imbalance raises low-back and shoulder risk over time | Replace Tue front squat with a hinge (deadlift 5×3 or clean pulls); add vertical pulling (pull-ups/C2B) to two metcons |
| High | Quad/knee stress stacked on consecutive days | Mon squat→Tue squat→ (rest)→Thu OHS→Fri squat = 4 squat-dominant days | Repeated knee/quad load with no recovery for that tissue; quality declines by Fri | Distribute: keep 2–3 squat-focus days, swap the others to hinge/pull/press |
| Medium | Metcon time domains clustered short | 3 of 4 metcons are 5–7 min glycolytic | Monotone energy-system exposure; aerobic base under-trained | Convert one metcon to a 15–20 min aerobic piece (already have Sat run — vary the engine, not just the legs) |
| Medium | Wall-ball volume very high across week | Mon thrusters + Tue/Fri wall balls + "Karen" 150 reps | Cumulative squat-to-overhead volume; shoulder + quad fatigue | Cap wall-ball reps mid-week if keeping "Karen" on Fri |
| Low | No planned overload retest | Squat percentages vary day to day with no progression logic | Strength gains are accidental, not driven | Pick one squat to progress with a retest date (see the weightlifting-progressions reference) |

## Top corrections
1. **Add the missing half of the body.** This is a squat program, not a CrossFit week. Introduce hinge (deadlift/clean) and pull (pull-up/row) — see the movement-pattern-taxonomy balance heuristics.
2. **Spread the knee load** so no more than 2–3 days are squat-dominant.
3. **Open up a time domain** — add one true aerobic piece.

## Revised example week
- Mon: Back squat 5×5 @ 80%; metcon — 21-15-9 thruster + **pull-up** (short, ~5 min)
- Tue: **Deadlift 5×3** @ ~75%; metcon — 15 min aerobic: row/run intervals (medium-long)
- Wed: Rest
- Thu: **Strict press 4×4** + **ring rows**; metcon — AMRAP 12 pistols + double-unders
- Fri: Back squat 3×3 @ 85%; metcon — "Karen" 150 wall balls (short–medium)
- Sat: Long run 5 km (long)

Now: push/pull and squat/hinge are balanced, knee load is spread, and the week spans short, medium-long, and long domains.

## Summary
The week is well-intentioned but pattern-skewed: heavy on squat, absent in pulling/hinge, and clustered in one time domain. The single most important fix is restoring pulling and hinge volume. Retest the chosen squat progression in 4 weeks; recheck weekly pattern balance.
