# Example 2 — Competitor near a season → bias to competition-specific

**Prompt:** "Quarterfinals are in 5 weeks. My weaknesses: heavy thrusters,
legless rope climbs, high-skill gymnastics under fatigue, and my snatch is
technically sloppy. What do I prioritize?"

**Context:** Experienced competitor, individual division, 5 weeks to a known event.

---

## Context recap
Hard deadline (5 weeks), competitive goal. **Urgency and competition-specificity
now dominate** — trainability within 5 weeks and likelihood-of-appearing matter
more than raw long-term benefit.

## Ranked priority table

| Item | Type | Benefit | Urgency | Trainability (5 wk) | Time cost | Fatigue cost | Transfer | Interference | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| High-skill gymnastics under fatigue | **Comp-specific** | High | High | Medium (skill+pacing) | Med | Med | High | Low | **Attack** |
| Heavy thrusters (capacity, not 1RM) | Comp-specific | High | High | Medium | Med | High | Med | Med | **Attack** |
| Legless rope climbs | Comp-specific | Med | High | Medium | Low | Med | Med | Low | **Attack (drill)** |
| Snatch technique (long-term) | General | Med | Low | Slow | Med | Low | Med | Med | **Defer past the event** |

## Symptom → root map
"Gymnastics under fatigue" is partly a **pacing/cycling** problem, not pure skill —
drill broken sets at competition-realistic heart rate, not just fresh reps.

## Attack now (1–3)
1. **High-skill gymnastics under fatigue** (most likely to appear and decide placing).
2. **Heavy-thruster capacity** (comp-specific, high benefit) — watch fatigue cost
   so it doesn't blunt gymnastics work.
3. **Legless rope climbs** as a low-cost drill alongside.

With 5 weeks, bias hard to **what the test will actually demand.** Do not start a
long-arc project now.

## Deliberately ignore (for now)
- **Snatch technique** — a slow-to-change, long-term project. Five weeks out, time
  spent here has low payoff and steals recovery from the comp-specific work.
  Revisit in the off-season.
