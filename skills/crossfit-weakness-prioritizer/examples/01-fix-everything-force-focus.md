# Example 1 — "I want to fix everything" → force prioritization, name root vs. symptom

**Prompt:** "I'm bad at basically everything: muscle-ups, chest-to-bar, my engine,
snatch technique, and double-unders. I want to bring it all up. Where do I start?"

**Context:** Recreational athlete, ~2 years training, no competition on the
calendar, trains 4 days/week. Goal is general fitness.

---

## Context recap
Broad-GPP goal, no deadline, moderate frequency (4 days). *Assumption:* no
competition pressure, so urgency is low and transfer/root matter most. Flag if a
competition is actually coming.

## Ranked priority table

| Item | Type | Benefit | Urgency | Trainability | Time cost | Fatigue cost | Transfer | Interference | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strict pulling strength | **Root** | High | Low | Medium | Med | Low | High (→ C2B, muscle-up) | Low | **Attack** |
| Muscle-ups | Visible (symptom of pull strength) | Med | Low | Slow | Med | Med | Med | Med | Follows root |
| Chest-to-bar | Visible (symptom of pull strength) | Med | Low | Medium | Low | Med | Med | Low | Follows root |
| Aerobic base | General | High | Low | Medium | Med | Low | High (all domains) | Low | **Attack** |
| Snatch technique | General/skill | Med | Low | Slow | Med | Low | Med | Med | Queue next |
| Double-unders | Distraction (low ceiling) | Low | Low | Fast | Low | Low | Low | Low | **Ignore for now** (fix with 5 min skill work, no block) |

## Symptom → root map
Muscle-ups and chest-to-bar are mostly **symptoms of the same root: strict
pulling strength.** Fix the root and both improve; do not run three separate
programs for them.

## Attack now (1–3)
1. **Strict pulling strength** (unlocks C2B and muscle-up together — highest transfer).
2. **Aerobic base** (broad transfer, low interference with pulling work).

Focus beats breadth here: 4 days/week and finite recovery mean two well-driven
adaptations will move faster than six diluted ones (coaching-principles §4–5).

## Deliberately ignore (for now)
- **Double-unders** — fast to fix with 5 minutes of skill practice; doesn't need a
  block and has low transfer. Don't spend a priority slot on it.
- **Muscle-ups / C2B as standalone goals** — they ride along with the pull-strength
  root; revisit once strict pull-ups climb.

## Handoff
Send the two chosen priorities to `crossfit-specialty-cycle-builder` (pulling
strength) or `crossfit-programming-cycle-builder` (to integrate pulling + aerobic
into one block).
