# Example 3 — Distinguishing a symptom (failing C2B) from the root (no strict pull)

**Prompt:** "I keep failing chest-to-bar pull-ups in workouts and it's wrecking my
scores. I just need to practice C2B more, right? Prioritize this for me."

**Context:** Intermediate athlete; can do 4 strict pull-ups, kips to the chin but
chest-to-bar falls apart after a few reps. No competition imminent.

---

## Context recap
One stated weakness (C2B) but the athlete has pre-diagnosed the fix as "more C2B
practice." Job here is to test that and rank the *real* target.

## Symptom → root map
**Failing C2B is the visible symptom; the likely root is insufficient strict
pulling strength.** 4 strict pull-ups is a thin base — under fatigue there isn't
enough strength reserve to pull the extra range to the chest, so reps fail. More
C2B volume on a weak base mostly adds grip fatigue and frustration, not capacity.

## Ranked priority table

| Item | Type | Benefit | Urgency | Trainability | Time cost | Fatigue cost | Transfer | Interference | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strict pulling strength | **Root** | High | Low | Medium | Med | Low | High (→ C2B, muscle-up, pulls) | Low | **Attack (the real target)** |
| C2B technique/volume | Visible symptom | Med | Low | Limited on a weak base | Med | High (grip) | Low until root improves | Med | Secondary drill, small dose |

## Attack now (1–3)
1. **Strict pulling strength** — build strict pull-up count (e.g. toward 8–10) with
   tempo/negatives/banded volume. C2B will improve *as a consequence*, plus the
   strength transfers to muscle-ups and heavy pulling.

Keep a **small** C2B technique dose (a few quality reps when fresh) so the skill
pattern stays grooved — but the priority is the root, not the symptom.

## Deliberately ignore (for now)
- **High-volume C2B "practice" under fatigue** — it's treating the symptom and will
  feel like work without moving the needle until strict strength comes up.

## Handoff
Send "build strict pulling strength → C2B" to `crossfit-specialty-cycle-builder`
(gymnastics-strength block) or `crossfit-movement-progression-builder` for the
strict-pull progression itself. If you want to confirm the root, a strict-pull
