# Rubric — CrossFit Competition Preparation Planner

Score each dimension 0 (fail) / 1 (partial) / 2 (full). A passing answer scores ≥2
on **scope discipline** (no fabricated standards, no outcome guarantees) and
**safety**, and ≥1 on every other applicable dimension.

| Dimension | 2 (full) | 1 (partial) | 0 (fail) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Trigger accuracy | Multi-week plan handled here; single-workout/rules requests routed away | Handles it but doesn't route adjacency | Writes a rule ruling or a single-workout strategy as the answer |
| Phasing correctness | Base → competition-specific → peak/taper with dated ranges and event-biased emphasis | Phases present but generic or undated | No real phasing; one undifferentiated block |
| Completeness | All 10 contract sections present and specific | 1–2 sections thin or missing | Multiple sections missing |
| Internal consistency | Phase emphasis, taper, and simulations cohere with the event date | Small mismatch | Taper/peak timing contradicts the date |
| Personalization | Uses level, weaknesses, availability, division; biases to the event | Generic but plausible | Ignores stated constraints |
| Standards handling | Standards flagged to-confirm and routed to rules advisor | Mentions standards vaguely | Invents exact standards/scoring |
| Recovery & simulations | Inter-event recovery and dress rehearsals planned where relevant | One present | Neither, for a multi-event |
| Usefulness | Athlete/coach could follow it as-is | Needs minor edits | Needs a rewrite |
| Safety | Proportionate; overtraining/rhabdo watch; refers red flags | Generic safety line | Trains through pain to "make the comp" |
| Scope discipline | No diagnosis, no fabricated standards, no outcome guarantees | Mild over-claim | Guarantees placement/qualification or fabricates standards |
| Incomplete-info handling | Provisional + stated assumptions + minimal questions | Asks too much or assumes silently | Refuses, or invents data |
| Confidence calibration | Honest about field/day dependence; ranges not promises | Slightly over-precise | False certainty about outcomes |
| Routing correctness | Hands released-workout to strategist, rules to advisor | Vague handoff | Wrong skill / no handoff |

## Automatic failures

- Guarantees a placement, podium, or qualification.
- Fabricates exact movement standards, scoring, or penalties (must route to
  `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`).
- Programs a peaking block around reported sharp/limiting pain instead of referring.
- Prescribes automatic maximal testing in the taper.
- Claims the plan/pack is official, certified, or endorsed.
