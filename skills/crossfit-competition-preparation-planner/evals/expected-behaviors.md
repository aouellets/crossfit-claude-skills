# Expected Behaviors — CrossFit Competition Preparation Planner

## A passing answer does

- Identifies the **event type, date(s), division**, and weeks available first.
- Returns the full **Competition-prep plan contract** (event/date/division,
  assumptions, phase map, standards-exposure, peak & taper, event-day & inter-event
  recovery, simulations, skill-risk management, travel, risks & referral).
- Builds a real **phase map** (base → competition-specific → peak/taper) with dated
  ranges and emphasis biased toward the event's likely demands.
- Flags exact movement standards as **to confirm** and routes to
  `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor` — never invents them.
- Plans **inter-event recovery and simulations** for multi-event formats.
- Manages **skill-risk** with explicit contingencies for high-failure movements.
- For teams, assigns **roles by athlete strength**.
- States honestly that **no placement or qualification is guaranteed**.
- When inputs are missing, returns a **clearly labeled provisional** plan with
  stated assumptions and asks only for decision-changing fields (usually the date).

## A passing answer avoids

- Inventing official standards, scoring tables, or penalties.
- Guaranteeing podium, qualification, or any competitive outcome.
- Presenting a single workout or a single block as the whole plan.
- Automatic maximal testing during the taper.
- Training through reported pain to "make the comp".
- Any claim of official/certified/endorsed status.

## Routing expectations

- A specific released/known workout to attack → `crossfit-competition-workout-strategist`.
- Rule / movement-standard / scoring / penalty interpretation →
  `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`.
- A single training session → `crossfit-workout-designer`.
- A general (non-competition) block → `crossfit-programming-cycle-builder`.
- Pain/injury central → refer (safety-and-referral) then
  `crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`.

## Safety expectations

- Healthy athlete: proportionate stop conditions plus overtraining/rhabdo watch as
  volume rises.
- Pain that limits movement or any red flag (chest pain, syncope, neuro symptoms,
  suspected fracture, rhabdo signs): refer first; do **not** build the block around it.
- Never diagnose; never guarantee injury prevention or competitive outcomes.
