# Example 2 — 12-week peak to a local multi-event throwdown

**Prompt:** "Build me a 12-week peak for a local one-day throwdown — 4 events in a
day. I'm a solid intermediate."

**Context:** Knows the venue runs 4 events with ~1–2 hours between them; weak event
is a heavy barbell complex; strong on engine. Trains 4–5 days/week.

---

## Event & date & division
Target: local one-day multi-event throwdown, 4 events, individual intermediate.
Weeks available: 12. Fixed checkpoint: event day with ~1–2 h inter-event windows.

## Assumptions
- 4 events, formats partly unknown → plan for breadth (one strength-biased, one
  short sprint, one long grind, one mixed) until the organizer publishes formats.
- 4–5 sessions/week. No injuries reported.
- Exact standards unknown → **confirm via `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`**
  once published.

## Phase map
- **Weeks 1–5 — Base/accumulation.** General capacity + targeted strength on the
  weak heavy-barbell complex (the limiter). 1 strength focus + 2 conditioning + 1
  mixed/week.
- **Weeks 6–9 — Competition-specific.** Train the four likely demand types; add
  back-to-back "two events in a session" to build inter-event recovery.
- **Weeks 10–12 — Peak/taper.** Reduce volume, hold sharpness, full event-day
  simulation in Week 11, deload into the event.

## Standards-exposure plan
Drill to confirmed depth/lockout/no-rep criteria for each published movement;
prioritize the barbell complex standard since it's the limiter. **Confirm exact
standards via `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor` before drilling.**

## Peak & taper
Volume down ~40% across Weeks 10–12; keep one sharp exposure to each demand type.
Protect the low back given the heavy complex; no maximal attempts in the last 10
days.

## Event-day & inter-event recovery
This is the crux of a one-day multi-event. Plan: fuel and rehydrate in each 1–2 h
window, light mobility, then a short re-warm-up before each event. Map a fueling
timeline across the day (easy carbs + fluid between events).

## Simulations
Week 11: full dress rehearsal — 4 events spaced like the real day, including
warm-up windows and fueling. Log how recovery held across events.

## Skill-risk management
The heavy complex is both a weakness and a no-rep risk under fatigue. Contingency:
rehearse a conservative opening weight and a planned re-attempt rather than maxing
cold.

## Travel
Local — scout the venue, confirm equipment brands/sizes, arrive early to claim
warm-up space and learn the floor.

## Risks & referral
Heavy-complex volume raises low-back risk; stop and refer for sharp/radiating pain.
**No placement is guaranteed.** Confirm standards via the rules advisor; detail the
strength block with `crossfit-programming-cycle-builder` and rank limiters with
`crossfit-weakness-prioritizer`.
