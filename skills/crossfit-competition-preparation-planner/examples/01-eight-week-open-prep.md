# Example 1 — 8-week Open prep (happy path)

**Prompt:** "I have 8 weeks until the Open. Intermediate athlete, individual
division. How should I train into it?"

**Context:** ~3 years training, can do chest-to-bar and 1 bar muscle-up, weak on
high-skill volume and the back-half of long metcons. Trains 5 days/week.

---

## Event & date & division
Target: the Open (online, 3 weeks of released workouts), individual intermediate
division. Weeks available: 8 (5 prep weeks + the 3 Open weeks). Fixed checkpoint:
each Open workout released weekly during the final 3 weeks, with a repeat window.

## Assumptions
- No injuries reported → standard load progression assumed.
- 5 sessions/week, ~60–75 min each.
- Exact movement standards for this season are **not yet confirmed** — see the
  standards-exposure plan.

## Phase map
- **Weeks 1–4 — Competition-specific base.** Build skill volume (gymnastics
  cycling under fatigue), engine for 8–20 min domains, and barbell-into-gymnastics
  transitions. 2 conditioning + 2 mixed-modal + 1 skill day/week.
- **Weeks 5–6 — Sharpening.** Shorter, higher-intensity pieces that mimic Open
  formats (couplets/triplets, ascending reps). Introduce repeat-attempt practice.
- **Weeks 7–8 (Open weeks) — Peak / execute.** Minimal new stimulus; each week:
  prime, perform the released workout, decide on a repeat, recover.

## Standards-exposure plan
Drill to **confirmed** standards only: chest-to-bar contact, bar muscle-up
lockout, thruster depth/lockout, double-under count. **Confirm the exact current
standards via `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor` for this season/division
before drilling** — do not practice guessed criteria.

## Peak & taper
Reduce volume ~30–40% entering Week 7; hold intensity and skill sharpness. Protect
shoulders and grip; no novel high-volume stimulus in the final 10 days.

## Event-day & inter-event recovery
Open workouts are weekly, so recovery is inter-day: prime well, fuel after, sleep,
and keep the mid-week sessions light during Open weeks.

## Simulations
In Weeks 5–6, run two full "release-day" rehearsals: warm-up window, one attempt,
score it, decide repeat, re-warm-up, second attempt. Practice the judging/standards
under your own counting.

## Skill-risk management
Bar muscle-ups are the high-failure movement at this level. Contingency: if a set
fails twice in a row, switch to fast singles; plan the rep-break before starting,
not mid-set.

## Travel
N/A (online). Set up a consistent home/box station, known equipment, stable
lighting for video standards.

## Risks & referral
Watch grip overuse and shoulder load from added gymnastics volume; if sharp joint
pain appears, back off and refer rather than push for the Open. **No outcome is
guaranteed** — this plan improves readiness, not placement. Confirm standards via
`crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`; detail each block with
`crossfit-programming-cycle-builder`.
