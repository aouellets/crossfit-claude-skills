# Example 3 — Team prep with role assignment (and refusing an outcome guarantee)

**Prompt:** "Prep our 4-person team for a team comp in 10 weeks and basically
guarantee we'll podium."

**Context:** Team of 4 (2 strong gymnasts, 1 strong lifter, 1 strong engine).
Mixed schedules; can do 2 team sessions + individual work weekly.

---

## On the "guarantee"
A plan can raise readiness; it **cannot guarantee a podium**. Outcomes depend on
the field, the events, and the day. This plan maximizes your preparation — no
placement is promised. (Honesty rule, see competition-strategy.md.)

## Event & date & division
Target: 4-person team competition, 10 weeks out. Fixed checkpoint: 2 team sessions
+ individual work weekly.

## Assumptions
- Team formats partly unknown → plan for relay, synchro, and split-work patterns.
- No injuries reported. Standards unknown → **confirm via
  `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`** once published.

## Phase map
- **Weeks 1–4 — Base + role discovery.** Individual capacity building; team
  sessions test who anchors which movement under team formats.
- **Weeks 5–8 — Competition-specific.** Drill relay handoffs, synchro reps, and
  work-split strategy for likely team formats.
- **Weeks 9–10 — Peak/taper.** Reduce volume, lock roles, rehearse full team
  events, deload into the comp.

## Standards-exposure plan
Drill confirmed standards (synchro lockout/depth criteria are common no-rep traps
in team events). **Confirm exact standards via the rules advisor** before drilling.

## Peak & taper
Team volume down ~35% in Weeks 9–10; keep handoff sharpness. Each athlete protects
their specialty.

## Event-day & inter-event recovery
Plan per-athlete fueling and recovery between events; the engine athlete may carry
back-to-back cardio events, so manage their inter-event recovery most carefully.

## Simulations
Week 9: full team-event dress rehearsal with handoffs, synchro, and floor layout.

## Skill-risk management
Assign **high-failure movements to the most reliable athlete** for that pattern;
keep a swap contingency if someone fails reps. Plan transitions, not just totals.

## Travel
Coordinate shared logistics, equipment familiarity, and a common warm-up window.

## Role assignment (draft, to refine in Weeks 1–4)
- Gymnasts → anchor high-skill gymnastics and synchro reps.
- Lifter → anchor heavy-barbell stations and openers.
- Engine athlete → anchor long cardio pieces and buy-in/buy-out runs.

## Risks & referral
Team formats add coordination load; refer any athlete with red flags before
continuing. **No podium is guaranteed.** Confirm standards via
`crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`; detail blocks with
`crossfit-programming-cycle-builder`.
