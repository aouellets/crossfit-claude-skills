# Example 1 — Back squat: knee cave + heels lifting (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Here's my back squat from a front-and-side angle. Knees cave at the
bottom and my heels come up. What's wrong and how do I fix it?"

**Context:** Side + front video, ~80% load, 3 reps shown, no pain reported.

---

## Observations
At the bottom third both knees track inward toward the midline; heels lift and
weight shifts onto the forefoot; torso pitches slightly forward as the hips rise
first out of the hole. Top positions look stable.

## What cannot be determined
From video I cannot determine *why* (ankle mobility vs. weak external rotators vs.
bar too heavy for this rep), the athlete's true ROM under lighter load, or
anything internal/anatomical. I'm seeing 3 reps at one load.

## Prioritized faults

| Fault | Observation vs. inference | Why it matters | Ease of correction | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Heels lifting / shift to forefoot | Observed | Safety + power leak; unstable base under load | Moderate (mobility/setup) | 1 |
| Knee cave (valgus) at bottom | Observed | Safety (knee) + lost drive | Moderate (cue + strength) | 2 |
| Hips rising first / forward pitch | Observed; *inference:* posterior-chain or bracing timing | Performance; turns squat into good-morning | Moderate | 3 |

## Cues
- Heels: "screw the feet into the floor, weight in the mid-foot/heel."
- Knee cave: "knees out, track over the middle toes."

## Drills
- Goblet squat to a target with a 2 s pause (groove mid-foot + knees-out).
- Banded squats (band around knees) for active external rotation.
- Ankle mobility screen + drills *if* the heel lift persists at lighter load
  (test the hypothesis — see movement-pattern-taxonomy).

## What improvement looks like
Heels stay down through the full descent; knees stay over the toes at the bottom;
hips and chest rise together out of the hole at the same load.

## Retest
Re-film the **same side + front angle at the same load** in 2–3 weeks. If the heel
lift only disappears when load drops, the limiter is likely strength/bracing at
that load, not pure ankle mobility — adjust accordingly.
