# Example 2 — Snatch swings out front

**Prompt:** "My snatch keeps swinging out away from me and I jump forward. Side
video. What's the fault?"

**Context:** Side-on video, 1 rep at ~85%, no pain.

---

## Observations
From the side: the bar travels away from the body off the floor and through the
knee, the athlete's weight shifts toward the toes during the second pull, the bar
loops out and forward at extension, and the feet land **ahead** of the start
position. The catch is reached but with a forward jump.

## What cannot be determined
From a single side rep I cannot confirm whether the root is setup (hips/shoulders),
first-pull patience, or simply this load. I can't see grip width clearly or
anything anatomical.

## Prioritized faults

| Fault | Observation vs. inference | Why it matters | Ease of correction | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bar loops out / away from body | Observed | Performance (long bar path) + control; missed lifts | Moderate (bar-path drills) | 1 |
| Weight shifts to toes in 2nd pull | Observed | Drives the forward jump; less power | Moderate (cue + position) | 2 |
| Jumping forward at extension | Observed; *inference:* downstream of the above two | Performance + consistency | Follows from 1–2 | 3 |

## Cues
- Bar path: "bar brushes the hips, keep it close — sweep it back to you."
- Balance: "stay over mid-foot through extension, push the floor straight down."

## Drills
- Snatch pulls @ 90–100% focusing on a close bar and staying flat-footed longer.
- Tall/muscle snatch to drill fast close elbows and a vertical path.
- Hang snatch from the hip to isolate extension without the floor's variables
  (see weightlifting-progressions).

## What improvement looks like
Bar stays close to the thigh through the second pull; weight stays over the
mid-foot; feet land roughly **in place** rather than forward; shorter, more
vertical bar path.

## Retest
Re-film the **same side angle at the same load** in 2–3 weeks; compare bar path and
foot landing. If it cleans up at lighter load only, the limiter is position under
load — keep technical work below the load where the loop returns.
