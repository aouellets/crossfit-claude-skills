# Example 2 — Minimal info, minimal equipment (provisional answer)

**Prompt:** "Give me a quick workout for today."

**Context:** No level, equipment, time, or injury info provided.

---

This is a **provisional** workout — I assumed: ~15 minutes available, a single pair
of dumbbells (most common home setup), and a healthy athlete with no injuries.
Tell me your time, equipment, level, and any niggles and I'll tailor it.

## Objective
Short, self-scaling full-body conditioning that works with one pair of dumbbells.

## Prescription
**3 rounds for time (cap 15):**
- 12 dumbbell thrusters
- 12 dumbbell renegade rows (6/side)
- 200 m run *or* 40 mountain climbers if no space

## Intended stimulus
Brisk but completeable — squat/press + pull + locomotion. Medium-short domain;
push the pace but you should finish every round without long rests.

## Target performance
**8–12 minutes** for a healthy intermediate with moderate dumbbells.

## Loading guidance
Pick a dumbbell weight you could press for ~15 strict reps fresh (RPE 6–7 for a
set of 12 thrusters). When in doubt, go lighter and move faster.

## Movement standards
Thruster: full squat, full overhead lockout. Renegade row: plank held, hips
square, elbow past the ribs.

## Scaling paths
- **RX-ish:** heavier dumbbells, run.
- **Intermediate:** moderate dumbbells, run or mountain climbers.
- **Beginner:** light dumbbells, drop renegade rows to bent-over rows from
  standing, march in place instead of running — same pattern mix, lower skill/
  intensity.
- **Masters / no run space:** mountain climbers or 30 s march; keep transitions
  short.

## Coaching focus
Keep the plank rigid on renegade rows (no hip rotation); break thrusters before
the legs give out.

## Risks & stop conditions
Stop for sharp shoulder/low-back pain or dizziness. Because key details are
unknown, defaults are conservative.

## Rationale
With unknown inputs, a dumbbell-only, RPE-loaded, easily-scaled piece is the
safest useful answer. The assumptions are stated so you can correct them.
