# Example 3 — Short sprint / power domain (stimulus discipline)

**Prompt:** "I want a short, fast, all-out workout — like under 5 minutes."

**Context:** Advanced athlete, full gym, no injuries, fresh (rest day yesterday).

---

## Objective
A true sprint stimulus: phosphagen-to-glycolytic, near-max output, redline
expected and acceptable because the piece is short and the athlete is fresh.

## Prescription
**For time (cap 5:00):**
- 21-15-9 of:
  - Calorie Echo bike
  - Burpees over the bar... *(no bar — make it burpees)* → **21-15-9 cal bike +
    burpees**

(Classic short couplet: bike calories + burpees, 21-15-9.)

## Intended stimulus
Sprint. Go out hard, hold on. Unbroken burpees, bike at a high but not blackout
pace on the round of 21, then empty the tank on 9. If you can talk during it, it's
too slow.

## Target performance
Advanced: **sub-4:00**. If it takes >5:00 it has become a medium-domain piece and
the stimulus was missed — reduce reps next time, don't extend the cap.

## Loading guidance
No external load — intensity is the variable. The "load" is pace; cue near-max RPE
(9–10) since the athlete is fresh and the domain is short.

## Movement standards
Burpee: chest to floor, full stand/jump at top. Bike: calories on the monitor.

## Scaling paths
- **RX:** as written.
- **Intermediate:** 18-12-6 to keep it under ~4:30 (preserves the sprint, not the
  exact reps).
- **Beginner:** 15-9-6 with step-back burpees — still a sprint, just shorter.
- **Masters:** reduce calories ~25% and allow step-back burpees; keep it short so
  the *intensity* (the point) is preserved.
- **Equipment alt:** rower or ski for the bike (slightly different calorie pace).

## Coaching focus
Commit to unbroken burpees on the 21 — hesitation, not fatigue, is what slows this
piece. Breathe at the top, not on the floor.

## Risks & stop conditions
Maximal short pieces spike heart rate fast: stop for chest pain, severe dyspnea,
or dizziness and seek evaluation. Only program this for an athlete who is fresh and
appropriately conditioned — not as a daily default.

## Rationale
Reps and cap are chosen so the athlete physically cannot turn it into a grind;
that protects the sprint stimulus the athlete asked for. Scaling reduces *volume*,
never lengthens the domain.
