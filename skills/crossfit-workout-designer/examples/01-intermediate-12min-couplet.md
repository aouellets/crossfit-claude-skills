# Example 1 — Intermediate 12-minute couplet (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Write me a ~12-minute workout for an intermediate athlete. Full gym."

**Context:** ~2 years training, no injuries, has barbell + rower.

---

## Objective
A medium-domain, pace-managed grind that builds the aerobic-to-glycolytic engine
while cycling a moderate barbell.

## Prescription
**AMRAP 12:**
- 10 power cleans (95/65 lb · 43/29 kg)
- 12 calorie row
- 15 wall-ball shots (20/14 lb to 10/9 ft)

## Intended stimulus
Sustained "hard but repeatable" effort — should *not* redline in the first 4
minutes. Target unbroken-to-2-sets on cleans and wall balls; row at a steady,
recoverable pace. Medium time domain (see time-domain-framework).

## Target performance
Intermediate: **6–8 rounds**. Under 5 rounds means it was too heavy or paced too
hot; over 9 means add load or reps.

## Loading guidance
Barbell should be ~50–55% of the athlete's power-clean 1RM (moderate, fast-cycling),
or RPE 6 for a touch-and-go set of 10. Wall ball to a standard target height.

## Movement standards
Power clean: full hip/knee extension, bar received above the knee (no squat clean
required). Row: monitor calories on the screen. Wall ball: hip crease below knee
at bottom, ball hits the target at top.

## Scaling paths
- **RX (above):** as written.
- **Intermediate:** 75/55 lb cleans, 10 cal row, 12 wall balls — keeps the same
  pacing and round count.
- **Beginner:** 65/45 lb (or empty bar to dumbbell), 8 cal row, 10 wall balls to a
  lower target — preserves the medium-domain grind without grip/skill failure.
- **Masters (55+):** reduce row calories ~20%, wall ball to a comfortable target;
  prioritize smooth transitions over load.
- **Equipment alt (no rower):** 15/12 cal bike or 200 m run; note running adds
  impact and removes the seated-recovery quality.

## Coaching focus
1. Smooth, early breaks on wall balls to protect the legs for the next round.
2. Touch-and-go cleans only while bar speed stays high — drop to fast singles
   before form degrades.

## Risks & stop conditions
Stop for sharp low-back or shoulder pain, dizziness, or loss of midline control on
cleans. Soreness next day is normal; severe pain or dark urine after this kind of
volume warrants medical evaluation (rhabdo caution).

## Rationale
A two-barbell-vs-engine couplet plus wall ball hits squat + hinge + monostructural
in a balanced way; the moderate load keeps the athlete in the intended aerobic-
glycolytic zone rather than turning it into a strength piece.
