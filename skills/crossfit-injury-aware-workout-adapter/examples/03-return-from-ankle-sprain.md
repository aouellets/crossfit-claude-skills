# Example 3 — Returning from an ankle sprain (graded reintroduction + stop conditions)

**Prompt:** "I rolled my ankle 3 weeks ago. The swelling's gone, I can walk and do
bodyweight squats pain-free, but I haven't run or jumped since. How do I ease back
into class?"

**Context:** Intermediate, was seen by a clinician who cleared walking and
bodyweight work, no current swelling or instability, full gym.

---

## Stated restriction / clinician limits
Lateral ankle sprain 3 weeks ago. Clinician cleared walking and bodyweight work;
running and jumping not yet trialed. Swelling resolved, pain-free in bodyweight
squats. Impact and lateral load are the unknowns to reintroduce gradually.

## Red-flag screen
Checked: **no** current swelling, **no** "giving way"/instability, **no** inability
to bear weight, **no** severe or unexplained pain. If the ankle gives way, swells
again, or becomes severely painful, stop and return to the clinician — that can
signal more than a simple sprain. (Per safety-and-referral.)

## Objective to preserve
Reintroduce impact and lateral demand **gradually** while keeping overall training,
so the engine and strength stay up and the ankle is loaded progressively rather than
shock-loaded by a high-impact metcon on day one.

## Movements to AVOID (early)
High-impact and high-demand ankle work until graded back: box jumps (especially
jumping down), double-unders at volume, heavy running, fast direction changes,
heavy single-leg balance under load, and pistols on the involved side.

## Allowed patterns
Low-impact engine (bike, row), bilateral squatting and hinging in a pain-free range,
upper-body work, and controlled calf/ankle loading. Step-ups before box jumps;
walking/jogging intervals before running for time.

## Range & loading constraints
Progress impact in steps: pain-free bodyweight → step-ups → low box step-down →
short jog → low box jumps → double-unders in small sets. Add **one** step at a time
and only if the previous step is pain-free during and the next day.

## Substitutions
- Running → bike/row to hold the conditioning stimulus without impact early.
- Box jumps → step-ups (step down, do not jump down) until impact is tolerated.
- Double-unders → single-unders in small sets, or bike, until pogo impact is fine.

## STOP conditions
Stop and reassess for: the ankle giving way, swelling returning, sharp or
increasing pain during or the next day, or a limp. Recurrent giving-way → back to
the clinician before progressing.

## Questions for the clinician
- Am I cleared to begin graded running and low-box jumping?
- Any balance/strength criteria I should hit before returning to plyometrics?
- Do you recommend bracing/taping during the return phase?

## Reassessment criteria
Advance one impact step roughly every few sessions, gated on being pain-free during
and the following day with no swelling. If a step provokes symptoms, drop back a
step. Persisting instability or repeat swelling → re-refer rather than keep
modifying around it.
