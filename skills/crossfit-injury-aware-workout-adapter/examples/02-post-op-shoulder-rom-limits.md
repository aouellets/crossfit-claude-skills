# Example 2 — Post-op shoulder with a surgeon's ROM limits (work strictly within them)

**Prompt:** "I'm 8 weeks post shoulder surgery. My surgeon cleared me for light
training but said no overhead and keep the shoulder below 90 degrees for now. How
do I train around it?"

**Context:** Intermediate, surgeon has given explicit limits, full gym, cleared for
light training.

---

## Stated restriction / clinician limits
8 weeks post-op shoulder. Surgeon's explicit limits: **no overhead**, **keep the
operated shoulder below 90° of elevation**, cleared for **light** training. These
limits are the hard boundary — this adaptation stays strictly inside them and does
not expand them.

## Red-flag screen
Checked: no new/worsening pain, no signs of infection (spreading redness, heat,
fever), no sudden loss of motion or instability. If any appear, stop and contact the
surgeon. (Per safety-and-referral.)

## Objective to preserve
Maintain overall fitness and train the rest of the body and the engine while the
shoulder follows the surgeon's protocol — no provocation of the operated joint.

## Movements to AVOID
Anything overhead or above 90° on the operated side: presses overhead, jerks,
snatches, pull-ups/muscle-ups, wall balls, kettlebell overhead work, overhead
carries, dips, and any pulling that drives the operated shoulder past the limit.

## Allowed patterns
Lower-body strength (squat/hinge that does not load the operated shoulder), engine
work that keeps the shoulder under 90° (bike, walking; rowing only if the surgeon
confirms it stays in range), core, and light, below-90° work on the operated side
**only** if the surgeon permits — otherwise train the uninvolved arm.

## Range & loading constraints
Operated shoulder: **below 90° elevation, no overhead, light loads only**, per the
surgeon. Do not test the boundary. Uninvolved limbs and lower body can train
normally within comfort.

## Substitutions
- Overhead pressing → omit on the operated side; press the uninvolved arm or
  substitute lower-body strength.
- Pull-ups → omit; substitute a below-90°, surgeon-cleared pulling option or train
  the lower body.
- Wall balls / overhead conditioning → substitute bike or below-shoulder-height
  conditioning to keep the engine stimulus.

## STOP conditions
Stop and contact the surgeon for: pain in the operated shoulder during or after,
any "catching"/instability, swelling, signs of infection, or loss of the range you
had. Do not push through to "test" the joint.

## Questions for the clinician
- May the operated arm do any light below-90° loaded work yet, and at what load?
- Is seated rowing within the current restriction?
- What is the timeline and the criteria for clearing above-90° and overhead?

## Reassessment criteria
Progress only on the surgeon's clearance and timeline, not by feel. Re-confirm ROM
and loading at each surgical follow-up before adding range or load on the operated
side. Anything outside the stated limits waits for explicit clearance.
