# Example 1 — Known couplet with confirmed standards (full strategy)

**Prompt:** "Here's the released workout with the official standards copied below.
21-15-9 thrusters (95/65 lb) and chest-to-bar pull-ups, 10-minute cap. Standards
(from the event packet I'm pasting): thruster = hip crease below knee at bottom,
full elbow + hip + knee lockout overhead; chest-to-bar = chest contacts the bar
below the collarbone, arms fully extended at the bottom. How do I attack it?"

**Context:** Athlete can do 95 lb thrusters unbroken to ~15, chest-to-bar in sets
of 8–10 fresh, grip fades fast. Event/season/division confirmed by the user.

---

## Confirmed workout description
As supplied and confirmed by the athlete: 21-15-9 thrusters (95/65 lb) +
chest-to-bar pull-ups, 10-min cap. Standards as pasted from the event packet
(thruster depth + overhead lockout; chest-to-bar contact below collarbone, full
extension at bottom). Treated as confirmed for this analysis.

## Interpretation
A classic sprint couplet — it rewards unbroken-or-near-unbroken gymnastics and
fast thruster cycling. The score is gated by how the chest-to-bar holds up as grip
and shoulders fatigue.

## Standards & no-rep risks
No-rep traps: thruster not reaching full overhead lockout when tired; chest-to-bar
contact too high (above collarbone) or arms not fully extended at the bottom. The
cheapest points are no-reps not given — finish each rep clean. **Confirm these
exact criteria with event staff/judges on the day.**

## Goal score
Grounded in this athlete: target **6:30–8:00**. Sub-6:30 requires the round of 15
chest-to-bar staying in ≤2 sets; if grip blows up early it drifts toward the cap.

## Bottlenecks
Chest-to-bar under fatigue (grip + lats). Thrusters are secondary but the round of
21 can spike the heart rate and wreck the first pull-up set.

## Rep-break plan
- Thrusters: 21 as 12/9 or 11/10; 15 as 8/7; 9 unbroken.
- Chest-to-bar: 21 as 8/7/6 from the start (break early — do not chase a big set);
  15 as 6/5/4; 9 as 5/4 or unbroken if grip allows.

## Pacing
Open controlled on the round of 21 thrusters — the trap is going unbroken and
arriving at the bar smoked. Push on the round of 9 of each.

## Transitions & setup
Chalk before you start and once at the round of 15. Bar set just under the pull-up
rig to minimize travel. Quick hands-to-bar; don't rest standing under the bar.

## Failure points & contingencies
If a chest-to-bar set fails twice, drop to fast singles immediately rather than
hanging and failing. If thruster lockout gets shaky, reset the front rack and
re-breathe rather than risk a no-rep.

## Repeat decision
If this is a repeatable event, only repeat if the first attempt was paced too
conservatively (finished fresh) — change the chest-to-bar break to larger early
sets. If you redlined, recover and keep the first score.

*Defer to `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor` for any dispute over what the
standard means.*
