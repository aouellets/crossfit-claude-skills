# Example 2 — Athlete hasn't supplied standards (conditional + ask)

**Prompt:** "There's a workout at my comp this weekend — something like 'a bunch of
toes-to-bar and dumbbell snatches, then a run.' How should I attack it?"

**Context:** Athlete is paraphrasing; has not supplied the official description,
rep counts, loads, cap, or movement standards.

---

## Confirmed workout description
**UNCONFIRMED → CONDITIONAL.** You've paraphrased ("a bunch of toes-to-bar and
dumbbell snatches, then a run"), but I don't have the official rep scheme, loads,
time cap, or movement standards. I will not invent them. Please paste the official
workout description and standards from the event packet so I can give a real
strategy.

**Please confirm with event staff / the packet:**
- Exact rep scheme and rounds.
- Dumbbell load and snatch standard (ground-to-overhead lockout, alternating?).
- Toes-to-bar standard (feet contact, full extension at bottom).
- Run distance and the time cap / scoring.

## Conditional interpretation (assumptions labeled)
*Assuming* a triplet of toes-to-bar + alternating dumbbell snatch + a medium run,
moderate volume, ~10–15 min: this rewards steady gymnastics that doesn't blow up
grip before the snatches, and a smart run pace.

## Conditional standards & no-rep risks
*If* standards are typical: toes-to-bar no-reps on incomplete extension at the
bottom or feet not clearly contacting; dumbbell snatch no-reps on soft overhead
lockout. **Confirm the real criteria — do not assume.**

## Conditional goal score
Can't set a grounded goal without rep counts, load, and your capacities. Once you
paste the workout and tell me your toes-to-bar set size and the dumbbell load
relative to your strength, I'll give a target range.

## Conditional rep-break plan
*If* toes-to-bar volume is high, break early (e.g. small consistent sets) to
protect grip for the snatches. Confirm the numbers first.

## Pacing / transitions / contingencies (conditional)
Run is the recovery/pace lever — settle there rather than sprinting into the next
round. Chalk before the dumbbell. If grip fades on toes-to-bar, switch to singles.

## What I need to finalize
The official description + standards (pasted), the load, the cap, and your relevant
capacities. Send those and I'll replace this conditional plan with a confirmed one.
For what the standard *means*, use `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`.
