# Example 3 — Repeat-attempt decision + an outdated standard (adversarial)

**Prompt:** "I did the released Open workout once and want to repeat it tomorrow.
Also, just apply the chest-to-bar standard from the 2019 rulebook I have — that's
the standard, right?"

**Context:** Repeatable Open workout; athlete finished their first attempt at the
cap, gymnastics fell apart in the final round. Quotes a 2019-season standard.

---

## On the 2019 standard (stale — do not silently apply)
A 2019-season standard may be **out of date**. Standards change by season, and I
won't apply an old-season standard as if it were current — that could cost you
no-reps on the day. **Confirm the current season's chest-to-bar standard** from
this year's official source (or via `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor`) before
we lock the rep strategy. Until you confirm it, the standards section below is
**conditional**.

## Confirmed workout description
You've confirmed the workout itself (your first-attempt score and structure).
Standards are **pending current confirmation** (see above).

## Interpretation
Repeatable Open piece; your first attempt hit the cap with the last gymnastics
round collapsing — so the score is gated by gymnastics durability, not by your top
end.

## Standards & no-rep risks (conditional until confirmed)
*Assuming* the current standard is similar to past chest-to-bar criteria (contact
below the collarbone, full extension at the bottom): your fatigue no-reps will come
from incomplete extension at the bottom. **Confirm the current criteria first.**

## Goal score
Beat your first attempt. Realistic gain: hold the gymnastics in planned small sets
the whole way instead of going big early and failing late.

## Bottleneck
Gymnastics durability in the back half — exactly where attempt one fell apart.

## Rep-break plan (the change for attempt two)
Switch from "big sets early" to **even, smaller sets from rep one** (e.g. consistent
3s/4s) so the final round doesn't collapse into failed reps and rest.

## Pacing / transitions
Open ~5% slower; the time you "lose" early you win back by never failing a set.
Chalk on schedule; minimize standing rest under the rig.

## Failure points & contingencies
If a set fails, go straight to singles — no hanging-and-failing.

## Repeat decision
**Repeat is justified**: your first attempt was a pacing/partitioning failure, not
a top-end-capacity ceiling, and you have recovery time. Change only the
partitioning and opening pace. If you were instead maxed out with clean even sets,
I'd advise keeping the score rather than risking a worse repeat.

*Confirm the current standard via `crossfit-rules-and-standards-advisor` before
the attempt.*
