# Example 1 — No rower: bike or run instead (happy path)

**Prompt:** "The workout calls for a 500 m row but I don't have a rower. I have an
Echo bike and open road. What do I do instead?"

**Context:** Intermediate athlete, the row is the engine piece in a triplet.

---

## Original demand profile (row)
- **Joint action:** hip/knee extension + horizontal pull (leg-drive then arm pull).
- **Force orientation:** mixed, seated.
- **Contraction / TUT:** fast cyclical, recoverable.
- **Musculature / energy system:** legs + posterior chain + some grip/back;
  aerobic-to-glycolytic engine.
- **Skill:** low-moderate (pacing technique).
- **Impact:** none (seated, no landing).

## Intended stimulus
A cyclical aerobic engine effort that lets the athlete recover the legs slightly
versus the loaded movements around it. Limiter is the engine, not impact or grip.

## Proposed substitution(s)
1. **Echo bike** — closest match for the engine and the seated, low-impact quality.
2. **Run** — acceptable if the bike is unavailable, but a bigger departure.

## What it preserves
- **Bike:** cyclical aerobic/glycolytic engine, seated low-impact recovery quality,
  leg involvement. Best overall match.
- **Run:** the engine and a similar time domain.

## What it does NOT reproduce
- **Bike:** loses the posterior-chain pull and the grip/back contribution of the
  row; the bike is more quad-dominant.
- **Run:** adds **impact** (landing/spinal loading) the row never had, removes the
  upper-body pull, and pacing by distance is less precise than calories.

## Loading / rep adjustment
Bike: substitute by **calories**, not meters — roughly **0.5–0.6 cal per meter** as
a starting ratio (≈ 25–30 cal for 500 m), then adjust to hold the same time.
Run: **400–500 m run ≈ 500 m row** in time; shorten if the impact is unwanted on
back-to-back days.
