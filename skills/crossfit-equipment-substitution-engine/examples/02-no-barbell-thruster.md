# Example 2 — No barbell for thrusters: dumbbells or odd object

**Prompt:** "Workout has barbell thrusters at 95 lb but I only have a pair of 35 lb
dumbbells and a sandbag. What do I use?"

**Context:** Intermediate, thrusters are the loaded movement driving the redline.

---

## Original demand profile (barbell thruster)
- **Joint action:** front-rack squat + vertical press (full-body, one piece).
- **Force orientation:** vertical, anterior-loaded.
- **Contraction / TUT:** explosive cyclical under fatigue.
- **Musculature / energy system:** legs + shoulders + midline; glycolytic.
- **Skill:** moderate (front-rack stability, timing the dip-drive).
- **Impact:** spinal/anterior loading; no landing.

## Intended stimulus
A full-body, lungs-and-legs redline where the front-rack load forces breaks. The
limiter is the combination of leg drive and overhead under fatigue.

## Proposed substitution(s)
1. **Dumbbell thrusters** (2 × 35 lb) — closest pattern: same squat-to-press,
   same anterior load, similar skill.
2. **Sandbag thruster / squat-to-press** — viable if you need more load than the
   dumbbells give.

## What it preserves
- **Dumbbells:** the squat-to-press pattern, vertical force, full-body glycolytic
  demand, and the "breaks under fatigue" quality.
- **Sandbag:** the load and the midline demand; can go heavier than the dumbbells.

## What it does NOT reproduce
- **Dumbbells:** independent arms change the rack and let one side compensate; at
  70 lb total vs. 95 lb the **absolute load is lighter**, so it will feel faster.
- **Sandbag:** awkward rack and shifting load change the skill and slow turnover;
  hard to cycle as fast as a barbell.

## Loading / rep adjustment
Dumbbells at 2 × 35 lb (70 lb) are lighter than 95 lb — keep reps as written and
expect faster rounds, or add 1–2 reps per round to restore the break pattern.
Sandbag: pick a weight that still forces a break every 7–10 reps; reduce reps
~15–20% to offset the slower cycle rate so the time domain holds.
