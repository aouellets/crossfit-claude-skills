# Example 2 — Gymnastics pulling specialty (strict → kip)

**Prompt:** "I want a focus block to get my first few strict pull-ups and then
chest-to-bar. ~6 weeks, 4 days a week."

**Context:** Can do 1 strict pull-up + 8 banded; kipping is sloppy. No injuries.

---

## Target & tradeoff
Priority: **vertical pulling capacity** (strict → kipping → chest-to-bar). Strength
and conditioning are maintained. Progress is **criterion-gated**, not calendar-
gated — see [../../../references/gymnastics-progressions.md](../../../references/gymnastics-progressions.md).

## Block structure
- **Weeks 1–2:** build strict base (eccentrics, scap work, ring rows, banded
  strict) — earn 3–5 strict before chasing kip volume.
- **Weeks 3–4:** introduce/clean up the kip once strict criterion is met.
- **Weeks 5–6:** kipping volume + first chest-to-bar attempts; retest.
- Light deload built into week 4 (drop one pulling exposure).

## Primary-quality prescription
Pulling **4 short exposures/week**, quality over quantity:
- **Strict-strength dose (2×/week):** 5×(3–5) negatives @ 3–5 s lower, or weighted
  ring rows; plus 3×8 scap pull-ups. Add a rep when the top set is clean.
- **Skill dose (2×/week):** hollow holds 4×20 s, kip swing drills 5×6, then small
  kipping sets *only* after the strict criterion is met.
- **Criterion gates:** ≥3 strict pull-ups + a 30 s hollow before kipping for
  volume; rhythmic kip before chest-to-bar attempts.

## Maintained CrossFit
Keep 3 metcons/week, but cap pulling volume inside them so total weekly pulling
stays manageable (avoid stacking high-rep pull-up metcons on dedicated pulling
days). Squat/strength continues at maintenance (1–2×/week, moderate).

## Weekly template (representative)
- **Mon:** Strict pulling → lower-body-biased metcon.
- **Tue:** Skill (kip drills + hollow) → monostructural conditioning.
- **Wed:** Rest / mobility.
- **Thu:** Strict pulling → maintenance squat.
- **Fri:** Skill + a mixed metcon with *limited* pulling reps.

## Interference management
- **Maintenance volume:** 3 metcons/week; total weekly pull-ups budgeted so the
  dedicated exposures stay high-quality.
- **Sequencing:** pulling work **fresh**, before grip-fatiguing conditioning.
- **Shared-fatigue conflicts:** don't put a high-rep pull-up/toes-to-bar metcon
  the day before a strict pulling session (grip + lat fatigue hides faults).
- **Accepted regression:** other gymnastics skills hold steady.
- **Deload:** week 4 lighter exposure.

## Retest & decision rule
Retest max strict pull-ups and first chest-to-bar at week 6. **Advance** to a
chest-to-bar/butterfly block if criteria met; **repeat** the strict phase if still
under 3 strict — do not force kipping on an unmet base.

## Risks & stop conditions
Stop on sharp shoulder/elbow pain or grip-tear risk. Banded/kipping volume on an
under-strength shoulder is the main risk — gate it on the strict criterion.

## Rationale
Building strict strength first, then earning the kip, then adding volume follows
the progression's prerequisite logic and prevents kipping faults from being baked
in under fatigue.
