# Example 1 — 8-week back-squat strength bias (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Build me an 8-week block to bring up my back squat. I train 5 days a
week and want to keep doing CrossFit."

**Context:** Intermediate, back-squat 1RM 140 kg, no injuries, full gym.

---

## Target & tradeoff
Priority: **back-squat absolute strength**. Everything else is **maintained, not
advanced** — conditioning and gymnastics hold steady; expect them to plateau (not
regress) for 8 weeks. This is the deal: you cannot peak squat strength and your
engine at the same time.

## Block structure
- **Weeks 1–3 — accumulation:** higher volume, moderate intensity (squat 2×/week).
- **Week 4 — deload:** ~60% volume, drop top sets.
- **Weeks 5–7 — intensification:** lower volume, higher intensity (heavy + a
  back-off).
- **Week 8 — realization/retest:** taper squat volume, retest a top single/triple.

## Primary-quality prescription
Intermediate → weekly undulation. Squat 2×/week.
- **Accumulation:** Day A 5×5 @ 72–75% (≈100–105 kg); Day B 4×6 @ 68% (≈95 kg).
  Add ~2.5 kg/week while bar speed and form hold.
- **Intensification:** Day A 5×3 @ 82–87% (≈115–122 kg); Day B 4×4 @ 75% back-off.
- **Realization:** work to a top triple @ RPE 9, then retest.
Accessories tied to the limiter (front-rack/midline or posterior chain), not
filler — see [../../../references/weightlifting-progressions.md](../../../references/weightlifting-progressions.md).

## Maintained CrossFit
Keep **3 metcons/week**, but cap the hard ones at 2 and bias them toward upper-body
/ monostructural so they don't compound squat-day leg fatigue. One pure
skill/aerobic day. No heavy leg-dominant metcons (heavy thrusters, high-volume
wall balls) on or adjacent to squat days.

## Weekly template (representative, intensification week)
- **Mon:** Squat Day A (heavy) → short upper-body metcon.
- **Tue:** Aerobic/skill (row + gymnastics), easy.
- **Wed:** Rest or active recovery.
- **Thu:** Squat Day B (back-off) → monostructural metcon.
- **Fri:** Mixed metcon (no heavy legs) + accessories.

## Interference management
- **Maintenance volume:** 3 metcons/week, ≤2 hard; engine holds, does not build.
- **Sequencing:** squat goes **first**, fresh; conditioning after or on separate
  days. Never a redline leg metcon the day before a heavy squat.
- **Shared-fatigue conflicts:** keep wall balls / box jumps / heavy thrusters away
  from squat days (compound knee/quad load).
- **Accepted regression:** conditioning PRs pause for 8 weeks.
- **Deload:** Week 4, and reduce all volume if bar speed drops or RPE creeps.

## Retest & decision rule
Week 8: build to a top single. **Advance** (run another intensification wave) if
you add ≥5 kg with clean form; **repeat/adjust** if you stall or form breaks.

## Risks & stop conditions
Stop a set on form breakdown, sharp knee/low-back pain, or bar-speed collapse.
Deload early if sleep/appetite/joint achiness signal overreaching.

## Rationale
Two weekly squat exposures with undulating volume/intensity and a planned
deload+taper drive measurable strength; capping and re-sequencing conditioning
keeps the engine intact without sabotaging recovery for the priority.
