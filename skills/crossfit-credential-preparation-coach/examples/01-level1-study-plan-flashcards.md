## Study plan (spaced repetition, weakest-first)
- **Week 1 — methodology + scaling (your gaps):** 3 sessions. Read/notes on the
  methodology, then build your own one-page "why" summary. Flashcards daily (below).
- **Week 2 — consolidate + safety + programming:** alternate methodology/scaling
  recall with safety and programming logic. Re-test week-1 flashcards (spacing).
- **Week 3 — full breadth + taper:** one original mock (below) early in the week,
  remediate misses, light flashcard review the last 2 days. No cramming day-before.

## Original practice items
**Flashcards (original — Q / A):**
1. Q: State the difference between *scaling* and *modifying* a workout. A: Scaling
   adjusts load/volume/complexity to preserve the intended stimulus for the
   athlete; modifying changes the movement itself (often for a restriction).
2. Q: Name the squat standard cue that confirms depth. A: Hip crease clearly below
   the top of the knee at the bottom.
3. Q: Why prioritize mechanics before consistency before intensity? A: Adding
   intensity to poor mechanics scales risk faster than fitness; you earn intensity
   with sound, repeatable mechanics.
4. Q: Give one valid scale for a pull-up for an athlete with zero strict pull-ups
   that preserves the pulling stimulus. A: Ring rows at a body angle that makes the
   target rep range hard-but-doable.

**Mini mock (week 3, 5 original items):** short-answer on methodology, one scaling
scenario, two standards items, one safety item. Self-grade, then remediate.

## Gap analysis + remediation
Methodology and scaling are your high-weight low-confidence domains — they get the
most week-1 time and the densest flashcard set. Re-rate confidence after the mock;
if scaling is still <4, add one more scenario block before exam day.
