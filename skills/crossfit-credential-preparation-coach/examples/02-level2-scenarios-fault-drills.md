# Example 2 — Level 2 applied-coaching scenarios + fault-ID drills

**Prompt:** "Getting ready for my Level 2. My weak spot is correcting faults live —
I see them but I freeze on what to say. Exam date not set yet."

**Context:** Coaching ~1 year; comfortable demoing and managing a class.

---

## Identified mode + exam timeline
**Mode:** Level 2 (applied coaching — seeing/correcting faults, cueing, lesson
delivery, class management, scaling decisions).
**Exam:** date TBD. **Plan:** provisional, organized as a 4-week block you can
restart when the date lands.

## Study plan (biased to your stated gap)
- **Weeks 1–2 — correction & cueing reps:** daily fault→correction→*one* cue drills
  (below). Practice out loud; one cue per fault, most-limiting fault first.
- **Week 3 — scaling decisions + lesson delivery:** scenario sets (below) under a
  60-second decision clock to fight the freeze.
- **Week 4 — integrate + taper:** mock applied scenarios end-to-end, remediate, then
  light review.

## Original practice items
**Fault-ID drills (original — fault → correction → single cue):**
1. Air squat: knees collapsing in. Correction: re-establish a stable tripod foot and
   external-rotation torque. Cue: "Knees out, screw your feet into the floor."
2. Push press: pressing before leg drive finishes. Correction: sequence dip-drive-
   then-press. Cue: "Drive, *then* press — let your legs throw it."
3. Deadlift: hips shooting up early. Correction: keep chest and hips rising together.
   Cue: "Chest and hips together — push the floor away."

**Applied-coaching scenarios (original):**
- "Mid-WOD an athlete's deadlift back rounds under fatigue and they want to keep
  going for time. What do you do?" Model answer: stop the rep, prioritize safety over
  score, re-cue or scale load/reps to restore mechanics, explain why briefly.
- "Class of 14, three clearly can't reach the Rx pull-up. Decide and communicate a
  scale in under a minute." Model answer: ring rows or banded at a hard-but-doable
  range, framed to preserve the pulling stimulus, delivered without singling anyone
  out.

## Gap analysis + remediation
Your bottleneck is *live correction*, not *seeing* faults — so the plan spends its
first half purely on the correction→cue link under time pressure. Re-rate after the
week-4 mock; if you still hesitate, add a daily timed "one fault, one cue" round.

## No-guarantee statement
These scenarios and drills are **original practice**, not the real exam, and this
prep **does not guarantee** you pass the Level 2 assessment.
