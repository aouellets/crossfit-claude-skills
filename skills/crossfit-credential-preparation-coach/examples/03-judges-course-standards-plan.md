# Example 3 — Judges Course, standards-focused plan

**Prompt:** "I'm taking the Judges Course before our local comp. I want to be solid
on movement standards and consistent no-rep calls."

**Context:** Will judge a local competition in ~2 weeks; experienced athlete, never
judged before.

---

**Mode:** Judges Course (movement standards, no-rep criteria, judging mechanics,

## Study plan
- **Week 1 — standards + no-rep lines:** for each common comp movement, write the
  full-ROM standard and the single most-missed no-rep trigger (drill below). Practice
  calling reps out loud against your own video or a training partner.
- **Week 2 — mechanics + consistency + taper:** positioning and clear communication
  drills; judge a partner's workout for consistency; light review before the event.

## Original practice items
**Standards / no-rep drills (original):**
1. Thruster: standard = bar starts from a front-rack squat, finishes at full
   lockout overhead with hips/knees extended. Most-missed no-rep: pressing out of a
   partial squat (no clear below-parallel) or soft elbows overhead. Call: "No rep —
   depth" / "No rep — finish overhead."
2. Toes-to-bar: standard = both feet start behind the bar, both touch the bar
   together at the top. No-rep: one foot, or feet not touching. Call: "No rep — both
   feet."
3. Chest-to-bar pull-up: standard = chest contacts the bar below the collarbone. No-
   rep: chin-only contact. Call: "No rep — chest."

**Consistency drill:** judge two athletes back-to-back on the same movement and
check that an identical rep gets an identical call — note any drift and correct it.

## Gap analysis + remediation
Your low-confidence cluster is no-rep calls, mechanics, and consistency — the plan
makes you *verbalize* calls early and judge a live partner before the event. After
week 1, if calls still feel hesitant, add a timed rep-calling round daily.

## No-guarantee statement
These drills are **original practice material**, not the actual course content, and
completing them **does not guarantee** you pass the Judges Course or judge flawlessly.
Always defer to the event's official, current standards.
