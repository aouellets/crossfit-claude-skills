# Example 1 — 8-week report: strength up, engine flat (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Here's 8 weeks of my log. Write me a progress report."

**Context:** Intermediate athlete. Log supplied:
- Attendance: 22 of 24 planned sessions.
- Back squat: 120 kg (wk 1) to 132.5 kg (wk 8). Deadlift: 150 to 160 kg.
- Strict pull-ups: 8 to 11 unbroken.
- 2k row: 7:48 (wk 1), 7:50 (wk 7). 1-mile run: not retested.
- Scaling: stopped scaling pull-ups around wk 5.
- Athlete feedback: "lifts feel strong, breathing workouts feel the same."

See [../../../references/coaching-principles.md](../../../references/coaching-principles.md)
for reading change as signal vs. noise.

---

## Period & data covered
**8 weeks (wk 1 to wk 8).** Present: attendance, strength (squat, deadlift),
gymnastics pulling, one monostructural retest (2k row), scaling history, athlete
feedback. Absent: weightlifting (snatch/clean & jerk), mixed-modal benchmark
scores, 1-mile run retest.

## Headline progress
Strength moved clearly (back squat 120 to 132.5 kg, +10.4%; deadlift 150 to 160 kg)
while the aerobic engine was effectively flat (2k row 7:48 to 7:50). This is a
strength-biased block doing what strength blocks do — the engine did not move
because nothing in the data trained it hard.

## What improved
- **Back squat: 120 to 132.5 kg (+12.5 kg / +10.4%).** High confidence — steady
  progression across the block, not a one-day spike.
- **Deadlift: 150 to 160 kg (+10 kg).** Moderate-high confidence.
- **Strict pull-ups: 8 to 11 unbroken,** and scaling dropped at wk 5. Consistent
  with the strength trend.

## What stalled or regressed
- **2k row: 7:48 to 7:50 — flat (within noise).** Stalled, not regressed. Only one
  retest, so confidence is moderate; treat as "no gain shown," not "lost fitness."
- **1-mile run / mixed-modal benchmarks: no retest in the period** — engine
  progress is otherwise unconfirmed.

## Consistency & adherence
Strong: 22 of 24 sessions (92%). Attendance fully supports the strength gains —
the numbers are credible.

## Scaling trend
Improving on the gymnastics side (pull-ups un-scaled from wk 5). No scaling-trend
signal on monostructural work in the supplied data.

## Actionable next priorities
1. **Add a dedicated aerobic stimulus** (e.g. 2 to 3 monostructural/interval
   pieces per week) if engine progress is a goal — the current log has none.
2. **Retest 2k row and add a 1-mile run baseline** so engine change is measurable.
3. **Keep the strength progression**, but watch that added engine volume does not
   stall the squat.

## What to retest next
2k row (4 to 6 weeks), 1-mile run (set baseline now, retest in 6 weeks), back
squat (after the next strength block). Hold deadlift retest until the next squat
retest to limit heavy testing.
