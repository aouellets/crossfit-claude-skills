# Example 3 — Flagging a regression worth a conversation

**Prompt:** "12-week report from my log, please."

**Context:** Intermediate athlete, goal is general fitness.
- Attendance: 30 of 36 sessions early, dropping to 4 of 12 in the last 4 weeks.
- Back squat: 140 kg (wk 1) to 145 kg (wk 6) to 132.5 kg (wk 12).
- 2k row: 7:30 to 7:28 (steady).
- Strict HSPU: 6 to 6 (flat).
- Athlete feedback (wk 10 to 12): "shoulders feel tight and tired, sleep has been
  bad, not motivated."

Apply the safety framework proportionately —
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).

---

## Period & data covered
**12 weeks.** Present: attendance, strength (squat), monostructural (2k row),
gymnastics pressing (HSPU), athlete feedback. Absent: weightlifting, mixed-modal
benchmark scores, deadlift/press.

## Headline progress
The first half trended up, but the **last 4 weeks show a real regression**: back
squat fell from a wk-6 peak of 145 kg to 132.5 kg (-8.6% off peak, and below the
wk-1 baseline), attendance collapsed (4 of 12 sessions), and the athlete reports
tight/tired shoulders, poor sleep, and low motivation. This is a pattern worth a
conversation, not a number to spin.

## What improved
- **First 6 weeks:** back squat 140 to 145 kg. Real but now eroded.
- **2k row: 7:30 to 7:28** — held steady across the whole block. The one bright spot.

## What stalled or regressed
- **Back squat regressed: 145 kg (wk 6) to 132.5 kg (wk 12),** ending below
  baseline. High confidence — it tracks with the attendance drop.
- **Strict HSPU: flat (6 to 6).** No progress.
- **The cluster of signals** — strength loss + attendance drop + poor sleep + low
  motivation + tired shoulders — reads like under-recovery / detraining, not a
  programming bug.

## Consistency & adherence
**Sharp decline.** Strong early (30 of 36), then 4 of 12 in the final 4 weeks. The
regression is almost certainly downstream of this, not independent of it.

## Scaling trend
No scaling-trend data supplied; cannot assess.

## Actionable next priorities
1. **Have the conversation first.** The honest read is detraining plus possible
   under-recovery — address attendance, sleep, and load before chasing the squat
   number.
2. **Note the shoulder signal.** "Tight and tired" with flat HSPU is a niggle to
   watch; if it becomes pain that limits movement, route to
   `crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter` and recommend evaluation — do not just
   program around it.
3. **Rebuild consistency** before re-testing strength; a deload/re-entry is more
   useful than a max attempt right now.

## What to retest next
Hold heavy squat testing until attendance is re-established (4+ weeks). Re-measure
back squat and HSPU after re-entry; keep 2k row as the stable anchor.

I am not spinning the squat drop as "just a deload week" — the supplied data shows
a regression, and the useful move is to name it and fix the root cause.
