# Example 2 — Sparse data, state the gaps (provisional report)

**Prompt:** "Give me a progress report for the last 6 weeks."

**Context:** Log supplied is thin:
- Attendance: not logged.
- Two named-workout scores: "Fran" 5:12 (wk 1, scaled — jumping pull-ups, 35 lb
  thruster) and one back squat session at 100 kg (wk 4).
- No baseline, no retests, no coach notes, no feedback.

This is a **provisional** report — most categories have no comparable data.

---

## Period & data covered
**6 weeks (wk 1 to wk 6), PROVISIONAL.** Present: two isolated data points — one
scaled "Fran" time and one back squat working load. Absent: attendance, any retest
of the same lift/benchmark, gymnastics, weightlifting, monostructural benchmarks,
coach notes, athlete feedback, and any baseline to compare against.

## Headline progress
**Cannot be computed from this data.** Progress requires two comparable points for
the same metric; the supplied log has one "Fran" score and one squat load, with no
second measurement of either. I can describe the snapshot, not the change.

## What improved
None confirmable — no metric was measured twice. The single 100 kg back squat and
the 5:12 scaled "Fran" are baselines for **next** time, not improvements.

## What stalled or regressed
Indeterminate. With no repeated measurement, neither stall nor regression can be
claimed without fabricating a comparison.

## Consistency & adherence
**Unknown — attendance was not logged.** This is the single biggest gap: without it
I cannot weight the credibility of any future PRs.

## Scaling trend
Only one scaled effort recorded ("Fran" with jumping pull-ups and 35 lb thrusters),
so there is no trend yet — just a starting scaling profile.

## Actionable next priorities
1. **Start logging attendance** — it is the cheapest, highest-value missing field.
2. **Retest one anchor benchmark** (re-run "Fran" at the same scaling) so the next
   report has a real before to after.
3. **Record back squat again** at week's end to establish a strength trend.

## What to retest next
"Fran" (same scaling) and back squat at the end of the next 4 to 6 weeks. Once two
points exist per metric, a real progress report becomes possible.

**One question that would most change this report:** do you have any earlier
baseline numbers (a prior "Fran", an older squat max) I could compare against?
