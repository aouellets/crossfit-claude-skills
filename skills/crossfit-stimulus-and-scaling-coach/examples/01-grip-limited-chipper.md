# Example 1 — Scaling a grip-limited chipper (preserve the grip stimulus)

**Prompt:** "How should a beginner scale this? For time: 50 toes-to-bar, 40
kettlebell swings (24 kg), 30 pull-ups, 20 hang power cleans (60 kg)."

**Context:** Beginner, can do 5 strict + a few kipping toes-to-bar, 8 kipping
pull-ups, no injuries.

---

## Identified stimulus
Medium-long chipper (~12–16 min). The defining demand is **grip and posterior-chain
endurance under accumulating fatigue** — every movement taxes the hands. "Done
right" = steady, managed breaks, grip fading but not failing catastrophically.

## Limiter
For this beginner: **grip and the toes-to-bar / pull-up volume** — they'll fail the
gymnastics long before the barbell. Grip is the whole point of the workout, so we
must scale to *keep them gripping hard*, not remove it.

## Levers chosen
**Volume** (cut reps to hold the time domain) + **skill** (regress gymnastics to a
version they can cycle) — both keep grip as the limiter. We deliberately do **not**
substitute the grip away.

## Scaling paths
- **RX (above):** as written. *Preserves:* full grip/posterior endurance. *Trades:*
  nothing — for an advanced athlete.
- **Intermediate:** 30 T2B (or knees-to-elbow), 30 swings, 20 pull-ups, 15 cleans
  @ 50 kg. *Preserves:* the grip-endurance chipper feel and time domain. *Trades:*
  total volume.
- **Beginner:** 30 hanging knee raises, 30 swings (16 kg), 20 ring rows (feet
  forward = harder), 15 hang power cleans @ empty/35 kg. *Preserves:* a hanging /
  pulling / hinge grip demand and the ~12–15 min domain. *Trades:* the bar-hang
  intensity, but **keeps grip as the limiter**.

## Masters note
Cap pull-up/T2B sets early to protect shoulders; reduce swing load before reducing
reps so the hinge volume (and grip) survives.

## Bad-scale warning
Swapping toes-to-bar for **sit-ups** and pull-ups for **ring rows on a bench**
makes it "easier" but **removes the grip and hanging stimulus entirely** — now it's
a midline/row workout, not the grip-endurance test it was designed to be. An easier
movement that deletes the limiter is a failed scale even if they finish faster.

## Risks & stop conditions
Stop on grip failure that risks dropping from the bar, sharp shoulder/low-back
pain, or form breakdown on the cleans. Soreness next day is normal; high-volume
novel grip work has rhabdo caution — severe pain or dark urine warrants evaluation.
