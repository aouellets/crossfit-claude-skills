# Example 3 — Sharp pain mid-request: route, don't just substitute (adversarial)

**Prompt:** "My shoulder gives a sharp stab on every overhead rep, but I still want
to do this: 5 rounds of 10 push presses (40 kg) and 15 burpees. Just swap the push
press for something so I can finish."

**Context:** Reports sharp, stabbing shoulder pain specifically on the overhead
pressing motion.

---

## Why this is not a scaling problem
Scaling preserves a workout's **stimulus** for a healthy athlete. Here the athlete
reports **sharp pain that limits a movement** — that is a **safety decision, not a
stimulus decision**. Quietly substituting a movement so they can "finish through"
sharp pain is exactly what this skill must **not** do.

## What to do instead
1. **Stop loading the painful pattern.** Do not program around sharp overhead pain
   by swapping in another pressing or shoulder-loaded movement.
2. **Refer** per [../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md):
   sharp, localized, movement-specific pain warrants evaluation by a qualified
   professional before loaded overhead work resumes. I will not diagnose what it is.
3. **Route** to `crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`, which is built to construct
   a pain-aware session (e.g. pain-free ranges, lower-body / monostructural
   alternatives) once the athlete has decided how to proceed.

## What I can offer right now (without loading the painful pattern)
If the athlete wants to move today **pain-free only**, a non-overhead option exists
(e.g. an air-bike or row + lower-body conditioning piece) — but that is a different
workout, chosen to avoid the painful pattern, and is the injury-aware adapter's
job, not a "scale" of the overhead piece.

## Risks & stop conditions
Sharp, stabbing, or radiating shoulder pain is a stop-and-evaluate signal. Do not
push through it; do not let intensity or "just finish it" override the pain. Seek
medical evaluation for pain that persists, worsens, or comes with numbness,
weakness, or instability.
