# Example 2 — A single retest to confirm a block worked (minimum battery)

**Prompt:** "I just finished an 8-week strength cycle focused on the back squat.
How do I check it actually worked? I don't want to test a bunch of stuff."

**Context:** Intermediate. Pre-cycle baseline: back squat 3RM = 120 kg (logged 8
weeks ago, depth to standard). No injuries.

---

## Question
Did the 8-week back-squat strength block produce a real increase in maximal
lower-body strength?

## Minimum battery
**One test: back squat 3RM retest.** *Measures:* maximal lower-body strength.
*Setup:* same rack/barbell, ramp to a heavy technically sound triple, RPE ~9.
*Scoring:* heaviest 3 reps to the same depth standard used at baseline.

One test is correct here. The cycle trained one quality; retesting only that quality
answers the question with minimal cost. Adding a 1RM, a metcon, or a row would add
fatigue and confound the comparison without informing the decision.

## Sequencing & rest
Standalone session, fully rested (≥48 h from hard legs). Thorough ramp-up; do not
pair with conditioning the same day.

## Baseline capture
You already have the baseline (120 kg, dated, same standard). Record the retest with
identical fields — date, load, depth, bodyweight, equipment — so the two numbers are
directly comparable.

## Retest schedule
This *is* the scheduled retest (8 weeks post-baseline). At this level, **+5 kg or
more** on the 3RM is a meaningful, worthwhile gain; a flat or sub-2.5 kg result means
the block underdelivered.

## How results change training
- **+5 kg or more:** the block worked — bank the gain, rotate to a new emphasis
  (engine or gymnastics) and re-baseline that quality.
- **Flat (±2.5 kg):** the stimulus, recovery, or adherence was insufficient — audit
  the block (route to `crossfit-programming-auditor`) before repeating it.

## Safety
Same-standard warm-up; stop for sharp knee/back pain or midline loss. Bail a failing
rep rather than grinding. Soreness from the cycle is not proof it worked — the retest
number is. See
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).
