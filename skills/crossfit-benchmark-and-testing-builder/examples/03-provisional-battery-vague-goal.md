# Example 3 — A provisional battery when the goal is vague (incomplete info)

**Prompt:** "Can you give me some benchmarks to test where I'm at?"

**Context:** No level stated, no equipment, no goal, no prior scores, no injury info.

---

This is a clearly labeled **provisional** plan. I have not fabricated any prior
scores or maxes, and I am asking only for the fields that would change the battery.

## Question (assumed)
*Provisional assumption:* you want a general baseline of where your fitness stands
across the three big qualities — engine, strength, gymnastics — with no single
competition or lift in focus. If your real question is narrower (e.g. "is my engine
holding me back?"), the battery shrinks to one or two tests.

## Minimum battery (conservative, sub-maximal where possible)
1. **500 m row OR 5-min max-cal bike (engine).** *Measures:* short aerobic-glycolytic
   capacity. *Setup:* whichever erg you have; record split/cals. *Scoring:* time or
   calories.
2. **5RM back squat (strength).** *Measures:* lower-body strength at a safer,
   self-limiting load than a 1RM. *Setup:* rack + barbell if available; ramp to a
   solid set of 5 at RPE ~8. *Scoring:* heaviest clean set of 5 to depth.
3. **Max strict push-ups or strict pull-ups (gymnastics).** *Measures:* relative
   pressing/pulling strength with no equipment dependency. *Scoring:* reps to full
   ROM.

I chose sub-maximal/self-limiting tests on purpose because I do not know your
training age or injury history — these de-risk the baseline without losing meaning.

## Sequencing & rest
Gymnastics fresh → rest → strength → erg last. Full rest between; split across two
days if you prefer.

## Baseline capture
Record date, score, units, equipment, and any scaling for each.

## Retest schedule
Provisional: engine in 4–6 weeks, strength in 8 weeks, gymnastics in 6 weeks; revise
once the real goal is known.

## How results change training
Once scored, the lowest relative result points to the first emphasis block — but
that ranking is `crossfit-weakness-prioritizer`'s job, and turning the scores into a
profile is `crossfit-athlete-assessment`'s.

## The two questions that would sharpen this
1. **What decision is this for?** (general baseline vs. a specific lift/benchmark/
   competition) — this can collapse the battery to one test.
2. **What equipment do you have, and any pain or injuries?** — this changes the test
   choices and the safety standard.

## Safety
Warm up before each test; stop for sharp joint pain, dizziness, or form breakdown. If
you have any current pain that changes how you move, do not test it — that routes to
`crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter` and the referral framework
([../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)).
