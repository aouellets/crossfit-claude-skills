# Example 1 — A 3-test engine + strength + gymnastics battery (happy path)

**Prompt:** "I want to baseline a new intermediate athlete across the big three:
engine, strength, gymnastics. Full gym. What should I test and how?"

**Context:** ~2 years training, no injuries, no prior recorded scores.

---

## Question
What are this athlete's current baselines in aerobic capacity, lower-body strength,
and pulling gymnastics, so future programming and progress are measurable?

## Minimum battery
Three tests — one per quality, each with a clean, repeatable standard:

1. **2k row (engine).** *Measures:* aerobic-glycolytic capacity and pacing
   discipline. *Setup:* Concept2, damper 4–6, monitor on 2000 m, record split.
   *Scoring:* total time + average /500 m split.
2. **Back squat 3RM (strength).** *Measures:* maximal lower-body strength without a
   full 1RM grind. *Setup:* rack + barbell, ramp to a heavy, technically sound triple
   (RPE ~9, leave one in the tank). *Scoring:* heaviest 3 reps to depth (hip crease
   below knee).
3. **Max strict pull-ups (gymnastics).** *Measures:* strict pulling strength /
   bodyweight ratio. *Setup:* rig, dead hang, no kip. *Scoring:* reps to full ROM
   (chin over bar, arms extended at bottom).

A 3RM rather than a 1RM is deliberate: it answers the strength question with far
less risk and is easy to retest.

## Sequencing & rest
Split-day if possible. If one session: strict pull-ups first (fresh), 8–10 min rest,
then back squat 3RM, then the 2k row last (most fatiguing, contaminates the others).
Full rest between each; this is testing, not a metcon.

## Baseline capture
Record per test: date, score (time/split, load, reps), bodyweight (for the pull-up
ratio), equipment, and any scaling. Store so the same conditions can be reproduced.

## Retest schedule
- 2k row: **retest in 6 weeks**; ≥3–5 s /500 m improvement counts as real.
- Back squat 3RM: **retest in 8 weeks**; +5 kg (≈10 lb) is a meaningful jump at this
  level.
- Strict pull-ups: **retest in 6 weeks**; +2 reps is meaningful.

## How results change training
- Slow row split with strong squat → bias the next block toward aerobic volume.
- Low strict pull-ups → add strict-pulling strength work before chasing kipping
  volume.
- Strong across all three → move to a GPP block and retest on schedule.

Hand the interpreted profile to `crossfit-athlete-assessment` and "what to fix
first" to `crossfit-weakness-prioritizer`.

## Safety
Warm up thoroughly before the squat and row. Stop for sharp joint pain, dizziness,
or form breakdown; on the 3RM, miss-by-bailing rather than grinding a failing rep.
