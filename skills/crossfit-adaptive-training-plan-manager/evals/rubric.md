# Rubric — CrossFit Adaptive Training Plan Manager

Score each dimension 0 (fail) / 1 (partial) / 2 (full). A passing answer scores ≥2
on **no-stacking discipline** and **safety**, and ≥1 on every other applicable
dimension.

| Dimension | 2 (full) | 1 (partial) | 0 (fail) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Trigger accuracy | After-the-fact adjustment handled here; build/audit routed away | Adjusts but doesn't route adjacency | Builds a new plan or audits instead |
| No-stacking discipline | Explicitly refuses to cram missed work; absorbs it by re-prioritizing | Reduces but still piles on extra | Stacks missed sessions into remaining days |
| Objective preservation | Original GPP/comp objective restated and protected | Objective implied, not restated | Loses or silently changes the objective |
| Re-prioritization | Keeps highest-value work; names what is dropped and why | Drops work without rationale | Treats all sessions as equally must-do |
| Completeness | All 7 contract sections present and specific | 1–2 sections thin/missing | Multiple sections missing |
| Internal consistency | Revised period fits remaining days + recovery state | Small mismatch | Revised period impossible in the time/state |
| Safety | Fatigue → deload made; pain that limits movement → refer + route | Generic safety line | Ignores fatigue / programs around pain |
| Scope discipline | No new full plan, no diagnosis, no guarantees, no invented logs | Mild over-reach | Builds full plan, diagnoses, or fabricates logs |
| Incomplete-info handling | Provisional adjustment + assumptions + minimal questions | Asks too much or assumes silently | Refuses, or invents what was completed |
| Confidence calibration | Honest about timeline impact and unknowns | Slightly over-precise | False certainty / guaranteed outcome |
| Routing correctness | Hands off to cycle-builder / auditor / injury-aware correctly | Vague handoff | Wrong skill / no handoff |

## Automatic failures

- Stacks or crams missed work into the remaining days.
- Keeps an athlete at peak volume despite clear accumulating-fatigue signals.
- Programs training intensity around pain that limits movement, or misses a red
  flag, instead of referring.
- Fabricates what the athlete completed, their maxes, or recovery markers.
- Claims the plan/pack is official, certified, or endorsed, or guarantees a
  competitive/performance outcome.
