# Example 3 — Athlete reports a niggle (refer + route, then adjust safely)

**Prompt:** "My shoulder's been catching and it's sharp on overhead since
Tuesday's jerks. My plan has heavy push press Thursday and a lot of overhead next
week. Just work around it and keep me on track."

**Context:** Objective is a 6-week GPP build, week 2. Upcoming work is
overhead-heavy.

---

## Safety first — this is a referral, not a "work around it"

You're describing **sharp pain that changes a movement** (overhead catching since
loaded jerks). That is not a niggle to program intensity around — per the
safety-and-referral framework,
the right first step is **a clinician evaluation of the shoulder before loading it
overhead again**. I won't prescribe heavy push press or an overhead-heavy week on
a shoulder that's sharp and catching, and I can't diagnose what's going on.

Routing: for training that respects a specific shoulder restriction once you have
guidance (or to substitute overhead work in the meantime), use
`crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter`.

## Objective restated
6-week GPP build, week 2. Goal stands; we adjust **away from** the symptomatic
pattern while you get the shoulder looked at — without stacking or forcing
overhead volume.

## What changed
- New **sharp overhead pain**, catching, since Tuesday's jerks (a red-flag-level
  movement change → refer).
- Schedule and other systems otherwise intact.

## Impact on the objective
**Minor, if handled now.** GPP is broad — we can keep building the other nine
general skills (squat, hinge, pull, engine, midline, gymnastics that don't load
the painful range) while the shoulder is assessed. Forcing overhead now risks a
longer layoff that would hurt the objective far more.

## Revised upcoming period (overhead removed pending evaluation)
- **Thu:** Replace heavy push press with **lower-body strength** (back squat or
  deadlift progression) + a **non-overhead** engine piece (row/bike intervals).
- **Next week:** Swap overhead-heavy metcons for hinge/pull/squat + monostructural
  conditioning; keep pain-free pressing **out** until cleared.
- Maintain time-domain spread so the build's conditioning stimulus continues.

## What was intentionally dropped (not stacked)
- All **overhead loading** is removed for now — not deferred into a later cramped
  block. We don't "make up" the missed overhead volume; we resume it only once the
  shoulder is cleared and pain-free.

## Adjustment rationale
Protecting the shoulder now preserves the 6-week objective better than pushing
through. Most of GPP can continue uninterrupted around a single restricted pattern.

## Recovery & referral notes
**See a clinician (sports physio/physician) about the catching shoulder before
overhead loading.** If you get red-flag signs (numbness/weakness down the arm,
the shoulder giving way, or rapidly increasing swelling), seek prompt evaluation.
Once you have guidance, hand the restriction to
`crossfit-injury-aware-workout-adapter` for a pattern-respecting plan.
