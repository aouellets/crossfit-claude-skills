# Example 1 — Missed 3 days traveling (redistribute, don't cram)

**Prompt:** "I was traveling and missed Mon/Tue/Wed of my build week. I'm back
Thursday. Fix the rest of the week — but don't make me cram it all in."

**Context:** Objective is a 6-week GPP build, currently week 3. Original Mon–Fri:
Mon back squat 5×3 progression + short metcon; Tue strict-press progression +
interval engine; Wed deadlift 5×3 + medium metcon; Thu skill (gymnastics) + long
aerobic; Fri "test" triplet. Athlete trains Thu–Sat this week (3 days left).

---

## Objective restated
6-week GPP build, week 3. Goal stands; we have **3 sessions left this week**
(Thu/Fri/Sat). The build is unharmed if we keep the highest-value work and let
the rest go — one disrupted week does not derail a 6-week block.

## What changed
- Missed: Mon (squat), Tue (press + interval), Wed (deadlift + medium metcon).
- No fatigue or pain reported — this is a schedule disruption, not a recovery one.
- Three training days remain.

## Impact on the objective
**On track.** The two progressing lifts (squat, deadlift) are the spine of the
build; the press and the Friday test are lower-value this week. We protect the
spine and drop the rest. We do **not** push the timeline.

## Revised upcoming period (Thu–Sat)
- **Thu:** Back squat 5×3 (this week's progression — the most important missed
  item) + short skill warm-up. Short metcon (~6 min).
- **Fri:** Deadlift 5×3 (second priority lift) + long aerobic piece (25–30 min)
  rolled in from the original Thu session.
- **Sat:** Strict-press progression (lighter, lower priority) + the original
  gymnastics skill work. Keep it moderate — first week back-to-back.

Time domains across the 3 days: short, long, skill/strength — still varied.

## What was intentionally dropped (not stacked)
- **The interval engine session** — its quality overlaps with Fri's aerobic work; redundant given the squeeze.
- **The Friday "test" triplet** — testing on a compressed, partly-detrained week gives a misleading number. Retest next week as scheduled.
- We did **not** add the 3 missed sessions onto 3 remaining days. Cramming 6 hard sessions into 3 days spikes fatigue and injury risk and degrades the quality of the lifts that actually matter — exactly the opposite of what the build needs.

## Adjustment rationale
A 6-week build tolerates one light week far better than a crammed one. Keeping the
two progressing lifts preserves the strength thread; dropping the redundant
interval and the premature test costs almost nothing toward the objective.

## Recovery & referral notes
First sessions back after travel — don't chase missed intensity. If Sat feels
unusually heavy, cut volume, don't add. No referral indicated (no pain/red flags).
