# Example 2 — Accumulating fatigue (deload decision)

**Prompt:** "I've done all my sessions but I'm wrecked — sleep's bad, my metcon
times are getting slower, and everything feels heavy. What do I do with next
week?"

**Context:** Objective is a 6-week competition build, currently end of week 4.
Plan called for week 5 to be the highest-volume week before a week-6 taper.

---

## Objective restated
6-week competition build, end of week 4, comp at the end of week 6. The objective
stands — but the **path** to it must change. The athlete is showing classic
non-functional overreaching signs (declining performance + poor sleep + global
heaviness despite full compliance).

## What changed
- All sessions completed (high compliance).
- **Performance declining** — metcon times slowing despite effort.
- **Recovery markers down** — poor sleep, everything feels heavy.
- No reported pain or red-flag symptoms.

## Impact on the objective
**Insert a deload now.** Pushing the planned highest-volume week 5 onto a
fatigued athlete will deepen the hole and arrive at the competition flat. Slower
times on full effort are a recovery signal, not a reason to train harder — see
coaching-principles §4 and §9. The competition date doesn't move, so we trade
volume for freshness.

## Revised upcoming period (week 5 — was peak volume, now deload)
- Cut **total volume ~40–50%** and **intensity ~20%** vs. the planned week 5.
- Keep movement patterns and time domains (so skills stay grooved) but shorten
  pieces and add full recovery between intervals.
- 2 lower-volume strength touches at RPE 6–7 (no maxes), 2 short skill/engine
  sessions, 1 full rest day added.
- Protect sleep: no high-CNS evening sessions this week.

## What was intentionally dropped (not stacked)
- The planned **peak-volume week 5** is removed, not deferred — we do **not** push
  that volume into week 6 (week 6 is the taper).
- The mid-week heavy double is dropped; recovery is the priority work this week.

## Adjustment rationale
You arrive at a competition fresh, not maximally fatigued. A deload here lets the
prior 4 weeks' work express itself. Skipping it risks under-performing or getting
hurt right before the goal event.

## Recovery & referral notes
Monitor: if times rebound and sleep normalizes within the deload, proceed to the
week-6 taper as planned. If global fatigue, poor sleep, or declining performance
**persist** beyond this deload, that is no longer ordinary overreaching — recommend
evaluation by a clinician before resuming hard training (see the
safety-and-referral reference).
