# Example 2 — Peri-workout fueling for a 2-a-day (timing focus)

**Prompt:** "I do a strength session at 6am and a metcon class at 6pm twice a week.
I'm dragging by the evening session. What and when should I eat around training?"

**Context:** ~70 kg, intermediate, no medical conditions, no disordered-eating
indicators. Goal is performance and recovery between the two sessions.

---

## Goal & current habits
Goal: hold quality across both daily sessions and recover between them. The gap
between sessions is the lever here — the issue reads as under-fueling across the
day, not a single meal.

## High-leverage habit targets
1. **Protein ~1.6–2.2 g/kg/day** (~115–155 g for ~70 kg) spread across the day so
   each session is bracketed by protein.
2. **Carbohydrate sized to a double day** — the higher end of your normal intake on
   2-a-day days to refill between sessions.
3. **Hydration target** across the whole day, not just at the gym.

## Meal timing around training
- **Before 6am:** small, fast carb if tolerated (banana, toast, sports drink) —
  optional if you train fasted well, but eat soon after.
- **Post-AM (breakfast):** protein + carb — the most important refuel for the
  evening session.
- **Lunch + a mid-afternoon snack:** carb + protein so you arrive at 6pm fueled.
- **Pre-PM (1–2 h):** lighter carb + a little protein.
- **Post-PM:** protein + carb before bed to support overnight recovery.

## Tracking method
A "bookend" check: one protein + carb feeding after the AM session and again before
the PM session. Two anchor feedings to verify on double days.

## Check-in & adjust cadence
Reassess in 2 weeks using evening-session quality and energy as the signal. If
you're still flat at 6pm, add carbohydrate to lunch/afternoon first — change one
input at a time.

## Scope note & when to refer
Coaching-level fueling guidance only. Persistent fatigue that nutrition and sleep
don't resolve warrants a clinician check (it is not always a fueling problem). See
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).
