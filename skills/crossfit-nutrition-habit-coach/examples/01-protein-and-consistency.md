# Example 1 — Busy athlete wanting more protein + consistency (happy path)

**Prompt:** "I train 4x/week and feel like my recovery is bad. I think I don't eat
enough protein and my eating is all over the place. Help me fix it without
counting every calorie."

**Context:** ~80 kg, intermediate, works full-time, no medical conditions, no
disordered-eating indicators reported. Eats little breakfast, big late dinner.

---

## Goal & current habits
Goal: better recovery and consistency without obsessive tracking. Current pattern:
under-eating early, protein clustered at one late meal, inconsistent day to day.
(No invented numbers — this is the athlete's own description.)

## High-leverage habit targets
1. **Protein ~1.6–2.0 g/kg/day** (~130–160 g for ~80 kg), spread across 3–4 meals
   so each has a palm-or-two of protein — not all at dinner.
2. **A plate template at each meal:** protein + 1–2 fists of vegetables/fruit + a
   carb sized to training that day + a thumb of fat.
3. **Eat a real breakfast** that includes protein (the single highest-leverage
   change for this athlete's current pattern).

## Meal timing around training
- Pre (1–2 h before): a carb + some protein you tolerate (e.g. oats + yogurt).
- Intra: water; electrolytes if sessions are long or sweaty.
- Post (within a couple of hours): protein + carb to support recovery.

## Tracking method
A simple daily checkmark: "protein at breakfast / lunch / dinner." Three boxes a
day. No calorie app required.

## Check-in & adjust cadence
Reassess in 2–3 weeks. If recovery and training quality improve, hold. If protein
is still landing mostly at dinner, add one easy protein source to breakfast and
recheck — change one thing at a time.

## Scope note & when to refer
This is coaching-level habit guidance, not medical nutrition therapy. If you have a
clinical condition affecting diet, or if food starts driving anxiety or rigid
rules, see a registered dietitian. See
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).
