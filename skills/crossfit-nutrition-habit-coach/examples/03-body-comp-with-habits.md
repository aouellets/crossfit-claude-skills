# Example 3 — Body-composition goal handled with habits (no crash, no moralizing)

**Prompt:** "I'd like to lean out a bit for summer but I don't want to wreck my
training. How should I approach it?"

**Context:** ~85 kg, intermediate, trains 4–5x/week, no medical conditions.
Screened: no disordered-eating indicators — goal is calm and performance-aware, no
food rules or compensatory exercise reported.

---

## Goal & current habits
Goal: a modest body-composition change while protecting training quality. Approached
as a small, sustainable adjustment — not a crash deficit, and with no "good/bad" or
"cheat" framing of foods.

## High-leverage habit targets
1. **Protein high and steady — ~1.8–2.2 g/kg** (~150–185 g for ~85 kg) to protect
   lean mass and manage appetite during a small deficit.
2. **Build most plates around protein + vegetables/fruit + a training-sized carb**,
   with fat to taste — volume and fiber keep you full.
3. **A small, gradual energy reduction** (modest, not aggressive) anchored to
   appetite and training quality — no fasting protocols, no "earning" food.

## Meal timing around training
Keep carbohydrate around your hard sessions so training and recovery stay protected;
take the small reduction mostly from the lower-demand parts of the day.

## Tracking method
Weekly: a single body metric trend (not daily weigh-ins) **plus** a training-quality
note. Two signals so you can tell fat loss from under-fueling.

## Check-in & adjust cadence
Reassess every 2–3 weeks. Aim for a gradual change. If training quality drops or
the trend stalls in a way that hurts sessions, add food back — performance is the
guardrail. Adjust one variable at a time.

## Scope note & when to refer
This is coaching-level habit guidance, not medical nutrition therapy. If a
body-composition goal ever starts driving anxiety, rigid food rules, compensatory
exercise, or rapid loss, stop the deficit and see a registered dietitian and, where
relevant, mental-health support. See
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).
