# Example 1 — Full profile from supplied benchmarks (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Assess me. Back squat 180 kg, deadlift 210 kg, clean & jerk 120 kg,
snatch 95 kg. 2k row 7:10. Fran 4:30 RX. 5 strict pull-ups, no muscle-ups,
unbroken 30 double-unders. Training ~4 years, 5 days/week, no injuries. Goal:
quarterfinals-level all-rounder."

**Context:** Athlete supplied a broad, mutually consistent data set.

---

## Snapshot
Strength-biased 4-year intermediate-to-advanced athlete; engine and high-skill
gymnastics are the limiters relative to an all-around quarterfinals goal.

## Profile by quality
- **Strength** — Strong. 180 kg back squat / 210 kg deadlift are well above the
  bar for the goal.
- **Power / weightlifting** — Good. C&J 120 / snatch 95 are solid; snatch trails
  the clean slightly (typical, worth noting).
- **Aerobic capacity** — Moderate. 2k row 7:10 is mid-pack, not a strength.
- **Anaerobic capacity** — Moderate-to-good. Fran 4:30 RX shows a workable
  glycolytic engine but room under load.
- **Gymnastics** — Limiter. 5 strict pull-ups and no muscle-ups is below the goal
  level; the root looks like strict pulling strength, not just kipping skill.
- **Mobility / movement quality** — **Unknown** (not supplied).
- **Skill** — Mixed: double-unders fine, no muscle-ups.
- **Work capacity / pacing** — Inferred moderate from Fran; **pacing discipline
  not directly evidenced**.
- **Consistency / recovery** — 5 days/week, no injuries reported; recovery inputs
  (sleep, stress, nutrition) **unknown**.
- **Goal-fit** — Strength clears the goal; engine and high-skill gymnastics do not.

## Evidence
Ratings rest on: the four supplied 1RMs, 2k row time, Fran score, the gymnastics
skill list, and stated training age/frequency. No data was invented.

## Strengths
Absolute strength; barbell cycling under moderate load; double-unders.

## Limiters
1. Strict/high-skill gymnastics (no muscle-ups; low strict pull-up count) — likely
   **root = strict pulling strength**, visible as the missing muscle-up.
2. Aerobic ceiling (2k row 7:10) relative to the goal.

## Unknowns
- Mobility and movement quality (no data).
- Pacing discipline across longer domains (only one short metcon supplied).
- Recovery capacity (sleep/stress/nutrition not given).
- Higher-skill ceilings beyond muscle-ups (e.g. HSPU, handstand walk).

## Ranked priorities (provisional)
1. Strict pulling strength → muscle-up. 2. Aerobic base. 3. Snatch technique.
Confirm benefit/interference order with `crossfit-weakness-prioritizer`.

## Recommended tests
- A longer monostructural test (e.g. 5k row or a 20-min AMRAP) to size the aerobic
  unknown.
- A strict-pull progression test (max strict pull-ups, then ring-row / banded
  capacity) to confirm the muscle-up root.
- A pressing-gymnastics check (max strict HSPU). Hand battery design to
  `crossfit-benchmark-and-testing-builder`.

## 4–12 week focus
8 weeks: strict pulling-strength block + muscle-up progression, with 2x/week
aerobic Z2 to lift the engine without stealing recovery from the strength work.

## Success metrics
Strict pull-ups 5 → 10; first strict or low-volume muscle-up; 2k row 7:10 → sub-7:00.
