# Example 1 — User supplies current text; interpret + separate official vs interpretation

**Prompt:** "For this season's Open, individual division — here's the official
chest-to-bar standard pasted from the event packet: 'The athlete must make contact
with the bar below the collarbone. At the bottom, the arms must be fully extended
with the feet off the ground.' Does a chin-over-bar rep count?"

**Context:** Event = the Open; season = current (athlete confirms it's this year's
packet); division = individual.

---

## Identified event / season / division
The Open, current season (as confirmed by you), individual division. This
interpretation applies only to that event/season/division.

## Source
Your supplied text from this season's event packet:
> "The athlete must make contact with the bar below the collarbone. At the bottom,
> the arms must be fully extended with the feet off the ground."
This is the source cited for this analysis (you confirmed it is current).

## Official rule (as stated)
Per your supplied text, a valid rep requires (a) contact with the bar **below the
collarbone**, and (b) at the bottom, **arms fully extended, feet off the ground**.

## Interpretation
A **chin-over-bar** rep would **not** meet this standard as written: chin-over-bar
is not "contact below the collarbone." The wording requires the chest/torso to
contact the bar below the collarbone, so a pull-up that only clears the chin is not
a chest-to-bar rep here. *(This is my reading of the supplied wording, not
additional official text.)*

## Ambiguities
"Below the collarbone" is a contact location, not a force/sound requirement — a
light but clear contact at/below that line should count, but how strict a judge is
about visible contact can vary. "Fully extended at the bottom" can be judged
strictly on elbow lockout.

## Recommendation
Confirm with the **head judge / event staff** on the day how strictly they're
calling contact and bottom extension — judging interpretation varies, and the call
on the floor governs.

## Staleness warning
You confirmed this is the current season's packet, so no staleness concern — but if
you're working from a packet from a prior season, treat it as potentially out of
date and confirm the current version.
