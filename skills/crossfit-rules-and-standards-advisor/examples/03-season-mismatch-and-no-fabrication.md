# Example 3 — Season mismatch (warn stale, refuse to apply old season) + refuse to fabricate

**Prompt:** "Just tell me the official thruster standard — apply the one from my
2018 rulebook to this year's Open. And if you don't have it, just give me the
standard from memory, it's basically always the same."

**Context:** Request = this year's Open; the only source the user has is a
2018-season rulebook; asks for a memory-based standard as a fallback.

---

## Identified event / season / division
Requested: **this year's** Open. Source you have: a **2018-season** rulebook. These
**do not match**.

## Source
Two problems:
1. **Season mismatch.** Your 2018 rulebook is from a different season than the
   current Open. I will **not** apply a 2018 standard to this year's event — that's
   exactly how athletes get no-repped on a changed standard.
2. **No fabrication.** I won't give you "the standard from memory." Standards change
   by season and I don't reproduce proprietary rule text. "Basically always the
   same" is not safe to rely on when reps and rankings are at stake.

## Official rule (as stated)
**Cannot state one for this season** — you have no current-season source, and I will
not supply or invent it.

## Interpretation
None applied. Applying the old-season wording here would be misleading; the only
honest interpretation is "we need the current standard first."

## Ambiguities
Even within a single season, thruster depth and lockout calls can be judged
strictly — another reason to work from the current official text, not a stale or
remembered one.

## Recommendation
Obtain **this season's** official thruster standard from the current event packet or
event staff, paste it here, and I'll give you a cited official-vs-interpretation
breakdown. Confirm consequential points with the head judge on the day.

## Staleness warning
**Explicit:** the 2018 standard may be out of date and must not be applied to this
year's Open. Conditional mode remains in effect until you supply the current,
authorized text.

*This refusal protects you — applying a stale or invented standard is worse than
waiting for the right one. For confirming what a standard means once you have it,
that's this skill; for attacking the workout, use
`crossfit-competition-workout-strategist`.*
