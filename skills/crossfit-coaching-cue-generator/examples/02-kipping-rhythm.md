# Example 2 — Cues for kipping pull-up rhythm

**Prompt:** "How do I cue a beginner to find rhythm on kipping pull-ups? They're
yanking with the arms and getting no swing."

**Context:** New to kipping, can do strict pull-ups, no shoulder pain.

---

## Target
Kipping pull-up — no hip drive / arm-dominant pull, missing the hollow-arch
rhythm.

## Working read
Coaching-level read: the athlete is initiating with the arms instead of the
kip swing; rhythm/timing problem, not strength (they already have a strict
pull-up). See gymnastics-progressions for the hollow/arch positions.

## Prioritized cues
1. **Rhythm cue, try first:** "Push the bar away, then pull it to you" — said in a
   beat to teach the arch-to-hollow swing before any pull.
2. **Backup:** "Hips to the bar, not chin to the bar" — redirects effort from the
   arms to the kip.

## Cue menu by type
- **Verbal:** "Swing first, pull last."
- **Visual:** demo the empty kip swing (no pull) so they see the rhythm isolated.
- **Tactile (ask consent first):** with permission, lightly guide the hips through
  one arch-to-hollow rep on a low bar.
- **External-focus:** "Make the bar bend / kick the wall in front of you."
- **Internal-focus:** "Squeeze ribs down at the front of the swing" (the hollow).

## Constraint or drill
Drill the hollow-to-arch swing on the bar with **zero** pull-ups for a full set —
remove the pull entirely so the athlete cannot cheat with the arms and must find
the swing. Then add one pull at the front of each swing.

## When to stop cueing
When the swing looks rhythmic and the hips lead, stop talking and let them string
2–3 unbroken. Beginners often lose rhythm the instant a coach adds a third cue —
one rhythm word per set is plenty.
