# Example 1 — Cues for knees caving in on the squat (happy path)

**Prompt:** "Give me cues to fix knees caving in on the way out of a back squat."

**Context:** Intermediate athlete, no pain, moderate load.

---

## Target
Back squat — knees collapsing inward (valgus) on the ascent out of the bottom.

## Working read
Coaching-level read: most often a motor-control/awareness issue (glutes not
driving abduction under load), sometimes loading too heavy to hold position. Not a
diagnosis — confirm by watching whether it appears only near max load.

## Prioritized cues
1. **External-focus, try first:** "Spread the floor apart with your feet." Said
   right before the drive out of the bottom. External cues land faster for this
   fault than "engage your glutes."
2. **Backup:** "Push your knees out to track over your pinky toes." Use if the
   floor-spread cue does not register.

## Cue menu by type
- **Verbal:** "Knees out as you stand."
- **Visual:** demo one rep with obvious knees-out, then one with the cave, and ask
  which felt stronger.
- **Tactile (ask consent first):** with permission, place your fingers on the
  outside of both knees so the athlete drives knees out into your hands.
- **External-focus:** "Spread the floor / screw your feet into the ground."
- **Internal-focus:** "Squeeze your glutes to push the knees out" (use only if the
  external cue does not connect).

## Constraint or drill
Loop a light band around the knees and have the athlete squat while resisting it
inward — the band makes "knees out" the path of least resistance without talking.

## When to stop cueing
Once two or three clean reps appear in a row, go quiet and let them accumulate
reps. Repeating the cue every rep makes the athlete coach-dependent; let the new
pattern groove on its own.
