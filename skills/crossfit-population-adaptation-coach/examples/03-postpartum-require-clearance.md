# Example 3 — Postpartum athlete (require clinician clearance)

**Prompt:** "An athlete is a few months postpartum and wants to get back into
CrossFit. How should I program for her?"

**Context:** Uncomplicated mention; no clinician clearance stated; previously
trained.

---

## Population & key considerations
A postpartum return is a **clearance-gated** situation, not an ordinary scaling
problem. Pelvic floor and core recovery, connective-tissue changes, sleep
deprivation, and (if breastfeeding) energy demands all change the right answer —
and an uncomplicated-sounding mention does not establish medical readiness. See
[../../../references/safety-and-referral.md](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).

## Clearance gate (REQUIRED first)
**Require the athlete's obstetric/medical provider's clearance to return to
exercise before programming.** Coach only **within** what that clearance permits.
Do not assume clearance from the absence of stated complications.

## What to adjust (only within clearance, conservative)
- **Volume/intensity:** start well below prior baseline; gradual reintroduction.
- **Impact:** minimize early (low-impact monostructural) until pelvic-floor
  readiness is confirmed by her provider.
- **Core/pressure:** avoid heavy bracing and high intra-abdominal pressure work
  until cleared; watch for coning/doming and leaking as stop-and-refer signs.
- **Recovery:** account for disrupted sleep; keep sessions short and restorative.

## What to emphasize
Reconnecting breathing/core mechanics, position quality, and a sustainable habit
within clearance — not chasing prior numbers.

## What to avoid or defer
Defer maximal lifting, high-impact plyometrics, and high-pressure midline work
until specifically cleared. **No max testing.**

## Scope limit & required professional guidance
This is coaching-level guidance and is **not** a medical clearance, pelvic-floor
assessment, or rehab plan. **Clinician clearance is REQUIRED**; a pelvic-floor
physiotherapy assessment is commonly valuable. Any bleeding, pain, fluid leakage,
dizziness, or blood-pressure concern → stop and refer immediately.

## Reassessment
Progress only on the provider's timeline and on demonstrated readiness (no
coning/leaking, sound positions). Re-refer if anything worsens or does not respond
as the clinician expected.
